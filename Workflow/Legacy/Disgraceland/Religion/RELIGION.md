# Overview of Religions in Disgraceland:



### **[[Church of the Ember Saint]]**
- **Core Beliefs:** This is the **wealthiest and most widespread faith** in Disgraceland. It reveres the **Ember Saint**, a legendary figure who braved fire to save hundreds during the Burning of Prattia, an ancient city. Followers believe that **suffering is a forge**, through which the faithful emerge stronger, and that fire purifies without destroying. **Service to others is sacred**, while hoarding wealth or power is considered heresy.
- **Practices & Traditions:** They follow the **Ember Book** of parables and conduct **Rites of Passing** where the dead are cremated and a single ember is given to the family. Monthly, they light communal fires on **Ember Day** for storytelling and sharing food, and hold a **Saint’s March** with lanterns on the anniversary of the Great Fire.
- **Symbols:** The **Burning Halo** (a golden ring of flame) and the **Unburned Hand** (a palm surrounded by stylized fire).
- **Structure:** Before the Water Wards they were led by the **High Flame (Pontiff)**, chosen from the **Council of Embers** (senior clergy), with **Redrobes** serving as priests/priestesses, and **Lay Faithful** as everyday followers. Today it's an unorganized religion.
- **Influence:** Their cathedrals, even if ruined, remain **symbols of hope**. Ember celebrations unite people, including non-believers. Figures like [[Bobby Cutman]], [[Dusty Dillon]], and the Murray family members (Marco, Rosa, Francis, Nico) are listed as followers.
- **Relationships:** They are **bitter rivals** with the [[Word of the Stillborn Star]] and **somewhat opposed** to the [[Faith of Mirella]] due to differing elemental outlooks (fire vs. earth). They have **strong mutual respect** with the Faith of [[El Santo]].

### **[[Faith of Mirella]]**
- **Core Beliefs:** This is an **ancient, secretive, and feared** faith tied to swamps, bogs, and shadowed waters. Followers believe the world itself recoiled from mankind's violence, and the sleeping goddess **Mirella (the Sleeping Mother)** waits for women to reclaim what was lost. **Men are considered dangerous, expendable, or cursed** by birth. The faith emphasizes **prophecy, visions, and blood rites**, believing Mirella’s dreams shape fate and her will is revealed through them.
- **Practices & Traditions:** Initiates wear **black veils** as a symbol of mourning, secrecy, and solidarity. They conduct **Dream Rites** involving herbs, chanting, and bloodletting, and perform an **Offering** on each new moon by letting blood fall into the swamp. The dead are returned to the earth in **unmarked graves** near water or roots.
- **Symbols:** The **Gold Bird** (a stylized golden bird on a black background) and **Tangled Roots** (marked in chalk or blood).
- **Structure:** Led by a **Prophet (currently [[Prophet Veerah|Veerah]])**, who is believed to channel Mirella’s will. Elite followers are the **Unseen Widows** (assassins, spies, ritual law keepers), with **Sisters** as ordinary faithful. Men are rarely trusted and never included in inner circles.
- **Influence:** Their influence spreads quietly, often among the desperate and wronged. **The Drownlands** is their largest and most sacred sanctuary. Even [[The Duke]]’s men avoid crossing the Widows. [[Prophet Veerah]] had a vision of "creatures of metal and flesh" that she interprets as a war Mirella wants the Widows to stop.
- **Relationships:** They **distrust** the Word of the Stillborn Star. They are **openly opposed** to the [[Church of the Ember Saint]]. They mostly **ignore** [[El Santo]]'s followers.

### **Faith of [[El Santo]]**
- **Core Beliefs:** More than a religion, it's the **pulse of Disgraceland’s working class, fighters, and survivors**. It honors grit, resilience, and justice earned with blood. **El Santo**, the Masked Saint, is believed to battle darkness in the underworld, with his spirit rising in every act of courage. **Justice is personal**, found in action, loyalty, and honor, rather than corrupt law. **Pain, bruises, scars, and broken bones are sacred marks** of survival.
- **Practices & Traditions:** Every follower owns a **mask**. After fights, combatants participate in the **Brotherhood of Bruises** to share drinks and bless wounds. They hold **Vigil Matches** where a champion fights for truth, and an annual **Day of the Last Bell** for remembrance and sparring. The **Noche de Lucha** tournament, held every four years, is the heart of their culture.
- **Symbols:** The **Mask** (white, silver, or gold) and **Bruised Knuckles**.
- **Structure:** The spiritual head is the **[[Grand Mascara]]**, with the **Grand Luchador** ([[Noche de Lucha]] champion) as a spiritual icon. The **Senior Enmascarados** are a secretive council who remain powerful figures in the religion.
- **Influence:** This faith is the **"glue" in [[Steeltown]]** and a beacon across Disgraceland, with even rivals showing up for Noche de Lucha. All training grounds and gyms are considered sacred. Many members of the [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]] follow this faith.
- **Relationships:** They are sometimes rivals, sometimes allies with the [[Word of the Stillborn Star]], and have **strong mutual respect** with the [[Church of the Ember Saint]]. They have little direct overlap with the Faith of Mirella.

### **[[Word of the Stillborn Star]]**
- **Core Beliefs:** This is the **oldest organized religion** in Disgraceland, dating back to ancient times. It revolves around **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star]]**, an ancient dead sun that slumbers beyond mortal sight. Believers see all things in cycles destined to fade and restart when Galliah awakens. **Silence is holy**, and truth is found in what is _not_ said. They believe in a **Great Reset** where Galliah will "ignite" and burn everything to ash to start a new cycle.
- **Practices & Traditions:** They observe the **Silence Hour** (meditation at dusk) and swear **Ash Oaths** over cold ashes. On the first new moon, they hold a **Star Vigil**, and adherents often take a second name with an "-ah" or "-en" suffix after the Rite of Stillness.
- **Symbols:** The **Unlit Star** (a black circle with a faint lavender halo) and **Chains of Renewal** (interlocking rings).
- **Structure:** Led by the **First Stillborn** (living prophet), who channels Galliah’s will and appoints **Speakers** (local religious heads).
- **Influence:** The **Disgraceland calendar was originally structured by Stillborn priests**, and many laws about contracts, death, and inheritance stem from their traditions. Many high-ranking Loyalists and figures associated with [[The Duke]], such as [[Emily Burton]], [[General Colt]], Mayor [[Gorren Brile]], [[Jacoban Clay]], [[Morris Gable]], [[Raynar Ventura]], [[Sgt. Rottwyler]], [[Soria Langston-Clay]], [[Tyren Will]], and [[Warren Scottyard]], are followers.
- **Relationships:** They are **bitter rivals** with the [[Church of the Ember Saint]]. They have **little direct conflict** with the [[Faith of Mirella]]. They view [[El Santo]]'s faith as a regional offshoot or heresy.

It's clear that while some religions are more common among the ruling class (Word of the Stillborn Star), others resonate more with the working class and those in rebellion (Faith of El Santo, Church of the Ember Saint).
