# 🥊 Faith of El Santo


> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎Church of El Santo
> ![[els.png|400]]  

> **"A bruise is a prayer the world can see."**


The **Faith of El Santo** is more than a religion—it’s the pulse of Disgraceland’s working class, fighters, and survivors. Inspired by the masked champion who became legend, El Santo’s followers honor grit, resilience, and justice earned with blood.  
Their faith lives in every gym, street fight, and act of stubborn courage. The beating heart of their culture is **[[Noche de Lucha]]**—the most celebrated event in Disgraceland.


## Core Beliefs

- **El Santo Lives:**  
  The Masked Saint never died—he battles darkness in the underworld, and his spirit rises in every act of courage.
- **Strength is Sacred:**  
  True strength is the power to lift others, not to dominate them.
- **Justice is Personal:**  
  The corrupt law is nothing. Justice is found in personal action, loyalty, and honor.
- **Pain is Proof:**  
  Bruises, scars, and broken bones are sacred marks—signs of survival and the soul’s resilience.

---

## Practices & Traditions

- **The Mask:**  
  Every follower owns a mask. Most are simple, but each is personal. Losing your mask is the greatest shame.
- **Brotherhood of Bruises:**  
  After fights, combatants gather to share drinks, bless wounds, and trade stories—scars are a badge of honor.
- **The Vigil Match:**  
  When justice demands, a champion is chosen to fight for truth in the ring. Winner decides the verdict.
- **Day of the Last Bell:**  
  An annual day of remembrance—bells ring for every soul lost, and all are invited to spar in their honor.

---

## Symbols

- **The Mask:**  
  White, silver, or gold, each mask is a symbol of identity, struggle, and hope.
- **Bruised Knuckles:**  
  Devotees often wrap their hands or tattoo their knuckles as a sign of their faith.

---

## Structure

- **Grand Luchador:**  
  The reigning champion of [[Noche de Lucha]]; spiritual head and icon for the next four years.
- **The Grand Mascara:**  
  The head of the faith. The spiritual leader of the El Santo religion. Upon death, a new Grand Mascara is chosen by the Senior Enmascarados.
- **The Senior Enmascarados:**  
  The secretive council who selects the tournament’s fighters and guides the faith’s traditions.
- **Ring Elders:**  
  Local leaders, keepers of custom, and mediators in disputes.
- **Santos (Saints):**  
  Ordinary faithful—anyone who stands up, fights on, and helps the fallen.

---

## Influence

- **Everywhere, for Everyone:**  
  El Santo’s faith is the glue in Steeltown and a beacon across Disgraceland. Even rivals show up for Noche de Lucha.
- **Gyms & Rings:**  
  All training grounds are sacred. Every gym is part sanctuary, part school, part court.
- **Justice in the Streets:**  
  Santo’s justice is swift and personal—handled in the ring, never left to corrupt officials.

---

## Holy Days

- **Noche de Lucha (every four years):**  
  The greatest tournament and festival in Disgraceland.
- **Day of the Last Bell (annually):**  
  Sparring and blessings for the departed.
- **Founding Match (spring equinox):**  
  Young faithful compete for the right to wear their first mask.

---

## Famous Quotes

- "Bruises fade. Honor stays."
- "If you can stand, you can fight."
- "The mask never leaves you, even when you take it off."
- "In the ring, truth can’t hide."

---

## Notable Figures

- **El Santo:**  
  The masked founder—legend says he fights evil beneath the world, waiting to rise again.
- **The Last Stillborn Champion:**  
  The only non-believer to win Noche de Lucha—now a folk hero and symbol of changing times.
- **The Senior Enmascarados:**  
  The faith’s shadowy council. Their names are known only to each other and the Grand Luchador.

---

## Relationship With Other Faiths

- **Word of the Stillborn Star:**  
  Sometimes rivals, sometimes allies—recent openness has bridged old divides.
- **Church of the Ember Saint:**  
  Strong mutual respect—fire and iron both endure, even when they clash.
- **Faith of Mirella:**  
  Little direct overlap, but women have fought and won in the ring—widows and sisters have left their mark.
