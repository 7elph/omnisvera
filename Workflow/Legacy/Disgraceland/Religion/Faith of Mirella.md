# 🜃 Faith of Mirella


> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎Faith of Mirella
> ![[fm.png|400]]  

> **"Beneath the mud, beneath the root, her dreams tangle through our veins."**

**Faith of Mirella** is ancient, secretive, and feared. It is the faith of swamps, bogs, and shadowed waters. Followers believe the world itself recoiled from the violence of mankind, and the sleeping goddess **Mirella** lies beneath the earth, waiting for women to reclaim what was lost.  
The faith is almost exclusively female—men are considered dangerous, expendable, or cursed by their birth. The Unseen Widows are its most visible hand in Disgraceland, but Mirella’s whisper travels farther and deeper than anyone admits.

## Overview



---

## Core Beliefs

- **Mirella, the Sleeping Mother:**  
  An ancient earth-goddess who slumbers beneath the swamps and roots. Her dreams shape fate, her will is revealed in blood and vine.
- **The Land Remembers:**  
  All wounds inflicted by men upon the earth and upon women are remembered. Mirella’s memory is long, and her vengeance slow but certain.
- **Prophecy of Reclamation:**  
  When the time is right, Mirella will rise and reclaim the world for women. Until then, her followers must act in secret, sowing chaos for the old order.
- **The Power of Blood and Dream:**  
  Prophecy, visions, and the rites of blood are central. Dreams are messages; omens are sought in birth, death, and the movement of animals.

---

## History

- **Old as Soil:**  
  The faith predates organized religion. Its rites and symbols are carved into the oldest stones, whispered in forgotten languages.
- **Persecuted and Hidden:**  
  Always feared, sometimes hunted, the faith went underground. Secret covens and hidden sanctuaries kept the old ways alive.
- **The Unseen Widows:**  
  In Disgraceland, the Widows are the faith’s enforcers, assassins, and prophets. Every action serves the dream of reclamation.

---

## Practices & Traditions

- **The Veil:**  
  Initiates wear black veils over the lower half of their faces. Only the Prophet may go unveiled. The veil is a symbol of mourning, secrecy, and solidarity.
- **Dream Rites:**  
  All major decisions and rituals begin with shared dreams—herbs, chanting, and bloodletting are common tools.
- **The Offering:**  
  On each new moon, followers cut their palms and let their blood fall into the swamp, feeding Mirella’s dreams.
- **Burial Rites:**  
  The dead are returned to the earth in unmarked graves, always near water or thick roots.

---

## Symbols

- **The Gold Bird:**  
  A stylized golden bird on a black background, worn over the heart—symbolizes Mirella’s watchful spirit and the freedom to come.
- **Tangled Roots:**  
  Marked in chalk or blood at gathering places, representing connection and secrecy.

---

## Structure

- **The Prophet (currently Veerah):**  
  The unquestioned leader, believed to channel Mirella’s will through prophetic dreams.
- **The Unseen Widows:**  
  Elite followers—assassins, spies, and keepers of ritual law.
- **Sisters:**  
  Ordinary faithful, responsible for raising daughters, spreading lore, and acting as Mirella’s eyes and hands.
- **Outsiders (Men):**  
  Rarely trusted; sometimes used, never included in inner circles.

---

## Influence

- **Whispers and Shadows:**  
  The faith is small but dangerous. Its influence spreads quietly, mostly among the desperate, wounded, and wronged.
- **The Drownlands:**  
  The largest, most sacred sanctuary in Disgraceland; the White Mansion sits hidden in the marsh, always guarded.
- **Fear and Respect:**  
  Even The Duke’s men avoid crossing the Widows if they can help it.

---

## Holy Days

- **Night of the White Veil (first full moon of spring):**  
  All faithful gather by water to renew their vows and honor the fallen.
- **Bloodwake (last new moon of the year):**  
  A secret ritual—each woman dreams alone, seeking Mirella’s guidance for the year ahead.
- **Reclamation’s Eve:**  
  A rare, dangerous night. Assassins strike targets chosen in prophecy.

---

## Famous Quotes

- "The swamp forgets nothing."
- "Dreams speak when tongues are cut."
- "Her vengeance rises through our daughters."

---

## Notable Figures

- **Veerah:**  
  Current Prophet; her visions are law.
- **The First Widow:**  
  Mythic founder; may never have existed.

---

## Relationship With Other Faiths

- **Word of the Stillborn Star:**  
  Distrusted. Too cold, too distant, too bound by old men’s words.
- **Church of the Ember Saint:**  
  Openly opposed—fire seeks to burn the swamp, but the swamp always returns.
- **El Santo:**  
  Mostly ignored; his followers are rarely women.
