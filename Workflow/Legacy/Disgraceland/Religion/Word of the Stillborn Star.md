# 🜨 Word of the Stillborn Star

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎Church of El Santo
> ![[ss.png|400]]  

> **"The silence between the stars is not emptiness. It is Galliah, watching, waiting, and remembering us all."**


**Word of the Stillborn Star** is the oldest organized religion in the world of Disgraceland. Its roots stretch back to the age of ancient cities and lost empires—when astronomers gazed up and wrote of a silent, failed sun named **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]**.  The faith is cold, patient, and relentless. Believers see all things as cycles, destined to fade and restart when **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]** finally awakens. Rituals are stark and quiet. Theirs is a god that speaks only through absence.

## Core Beliefs

- **Galliah, the Stillborn Star:**  
  A celestial body that never ignited—trapped between birth and death. **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]** is both god and warning, a promise that everything ends and begins again.
- **The Echo of Silence:**  
  Silence is holy. The world is full of lies and noise, but truth is found in what is *not* said. Temples and homes of the faithful are austere and eerily quiet.
- **The Great Reset:**  
  One day, when corruption overtakes the world, **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]** will “ignite,” burning everything to ash and starting the cycle anew.
- **Prophecy of Return:**  
  When omens align, the First Stillborn (the living prophet) will lead the faithful to “witness the Dawnless Light”—the sign of Galliah’s return.

---

## History

- **Founded by Mesh'den:**  
  Once a lonely astronomer-priest, Mesh'den claimed to hear **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]**’s voice in the silence. His followers, called **Stillborns**, swept through ancient cities and unified warring tribes with their chilling faith.
- **First Religion of the Old World:**  
  For centuries, it was the dominant faith. Many early texts, city laws, and holidays are rooted in Stillborn doctrine.
- **Decline and Survival:**  
  Overtaken in influence by the Ember Church and others, Stillborns are now a minority, but remain respected (and sometimes feared) for their age and mystery.

---

## Practices & Traditions

- **The Silence Hour:**  
  At dusk, followers meditate in silence, facing the eastern sky.
- **Ash Oath:**  
  Vows are sworn over cold ashes—a reminder that all things fade.
- **Star Vigil:**  
  On the first new moon of each year, all Stillborns gather for a night-long silent vigil, watching the sky for Galliah’s sign.
- **Names of the Faithful:**  
  Most adherents take a second name in old tongue (“-ah” or “-en” suffix) after completing the Rite of Stillness.

---

## Symbols

- **The Unlit Star:**  
  A black circle with a faint lavender halo—**[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]**’s mark.
- **Chains of Renewal:**  
  Interlocking rings, symbolizing endless cycles.

---

## Structure

- **First Stillborn:**  
  The living prophet, said to channel **[[Galliah, the Stillborn Star|Galliah]]**’s will. The First Stillborn leads all major rites and appoints regional Speakers.
- **Speakers:**  
  Local religious heads, guardians of doctrine and silent keepers of the faith.
- **Lay Stillborns:**  
  Everyday followers; expected to maintain silence, charity, and ritual observance.

---

## Influence

- **Law & Time:**  
  The Disgraceland calendar was originally structured by Stillborn priests. Many laws about contracts, death, and inheritance come from their traditions.
- **Temples:**  
  Ancient, spartan buildings—many now ruined or repurposed by newer faiths.

---

## Holy Days

- **Day of Quiet (last day of the year):**  
  All work stops at dusk. The world is meant to fall silent—no music, no fire, no words—until dawn.
- **Vigil of Ashes (midyear):**  
  The faithful gather, burn old regrets and tokens, and let the ashes scatter at sunrise.

## Relationship With Other Faiths

- **Church of the Ember Saint:**  
  Bitter rivals in the old world—Ember preaches action, Stillborns preach patience.
