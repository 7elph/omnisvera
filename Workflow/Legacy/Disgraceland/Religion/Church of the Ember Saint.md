# 🔥 Church of the Ember Saint


> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎Church of the Ember Saint
> ![[es.png|400]]  

> **"Through the flames, we rise. Through the ashes, we endure."**

The **Church of the Ember Saint** is the wealthiest and most widespread faith in Disgraceland’s modern era. Born in the ashes of an ancient city, the church reveres the legendary figure who braved the flames to save all he could—known simply as the **Ember Saint**.  
Wherever there is fire, there is hope. Followers believe suffering is a forge, and the faithful emerge stronger for it. In public, Ember Saints are grand and generous. In private, they are hard as iron.


## Core Beliefs

- **The Ember Saint:**  
  The first and greatest of the faithful, the Ember Saint walked through fire untouched, saving hundreds during the great blaze of Prattia. His deeds are both myth and moral code.
- **The Flame Endures:**  
  Fire purifies, but it does not destroy. Each trial faced is a test, a chance to be reforged.
- **Service is Salvation:**  
  To help others is sacred. To hoard wealth or power is heresy.
- **Ashes Remember:**  
  The past is not forgotten. Every ember is a spark of memory, kept alive through story and ritual.

---

## History

- **The Burning of Prattia:**  
  Legends tell of a fire that nearly destroyed the greatest city of the old world. The Ember Saint emerged, saving lives, guiding the lost, and inspiring a movement.
- **Rise to Power:**  
  The faith spread through charity and spectacle. Ornate cathedrals rose, and the faith’s leaders became rich and influential.
- **Survival and Renewal:**  
  The Church survived wars, disasters, and purges. Even now, after so much has been lost, their temples (ruined or not) remain symbols of hope.

---

## Practices & Traditions

- **The Ember Book:**  
  Holy scripture containing parables of the Ember Saint’s deeds and teachings. Each tale ends with a question or lesson for the reader.
- **Rites of Passing:**  
  The dead are cremated, and a single ember is given to their family. The flame symbolizes the spirit’s endurance.
- **Day of Ember:**  
  Once a month, believers light a communal fire at dusk, sharing stories and food with neighbors and strangers alike.
- **Saint’s March:**  
  On the anniversary of the Great Fire, followers parade through the city with lanterns, chanting and singing hymns until sunrise.

---

## Symbols

- **The Burning Halo:**  
  A golden ring of flame, often painted above doors or tattooed on the chest.
- **The Unburned Hand:**  
  A palm surrounded by stylized fire, representing protection and sacrifice.

---

## Structure

- **High Flame (Pontiff):**  
  The supreme leader of the church, chosen from the Council of Embers.
- **Council of Embers:**  
  Senior clergy who oversee regions, temples, and doctrine.
- **Redrobes:**  
  Priests and priestesses who serve the faith, recognized by their long, hooded ember-red robes.
- **Lay Faithful:**  
  Everyday followers, often organized into charitable guilds and work crews.

---

## Influence

- **Wealth:**  
  The church controls enormous wealth, much of it redistributed through charity—but some suspect greed and corruption at the top.
- **Architecture:**  
  Their cathedrals are renowned for marble, glass, and gold—most now ruined or gutted, but a few remain standing as beacons.
- **Festivals:**  
  Ember celebrations bring people together, even nonbelievers.

---

## Holy Days

- **Ember Day (monthly):**  
  A night of communal fire, feasting, and storytelling.
- **Saint’s Vigil (anniversary of the Great Fire):**  
  All-night procession, ending at dawn with the lighting of the Grand Flame.
- **The Forging:**  
  Every five years, followers gather for a mass ritual of renewal and oaths—new Redrobes are anointed here.
