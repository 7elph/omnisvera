# THE DROWNLANDS

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎A SWAMP IN THE DROWNLANDS
> ![[drown.png|400]]  

A place older than the fall.

The Drownlands slither in the lowlands east of Ashmoor, a haunted quilt of drowned trees, rotted boardwalks, and fog that never lifts. Some call it a swamp, but that word doesn't do it justice. It's a breathing thing — a tangled forest of gnarled cypress and ash, their roots strangled in black water, their limbs hung with funeral moss. The air smells of wet bark, fungal rot, and something sweet underneath, like the perfume of something long dead still clinging to its bones.

Sunlight barely filters through the canopy here. Instead, the world is lit in pale greens and grays, reflections of half-light on slick water. Frogs croak like old engines. Insects hum in strange rhythms. Every so often, you might hear the slap of something large slipping beneath the surface — and if you're smart, you don't go looking.

The Unseen Widows rule these parts. Women clad in black, silent and swift, they watch from the shadows, veiled faces peeking between the moss. Most folks believe they’re witches, or ghosts, or both. But the Widows follow one woman only — **Veerah**, their Prophet. They say she sees the future in her sleep. They say her dreams come true. They say no man who walks into the heart of the Drownlands returns unchanged… if he returns at all.

People steer clear, and for good reason. The land doesn’t welcome strangers. Paths vanish. Trees move. Your voice doesn’t echo here, it sinks. The Drownlands aren’t just a place. They’re a warning.

And they remember you.
