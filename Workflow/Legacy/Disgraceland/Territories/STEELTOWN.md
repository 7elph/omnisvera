# STEELTOWN

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎STEELTOWN
> ![[s5t.png|400]]  

Steeltown is a rustbucket miracle. A defiant patchwork of soot-streaked buildings and stubborn souls holding tight to dignity with oil-stained hands. Once the fire-belching pride of pre-Tetra industry, this was where steel was born and rails were fed across the continent. Now? It's a smoldering husk of its former self… and still somehow, impossibly, alive.

The chimneys still cough black smoke like dying lungs. Oil drums burn on every corner just to beat back the frost, and market stalls burst out between rusted scaffolds and slag piles like weeds through concrete. What little industry survives does so through spit, weld, and willpower, jury-rigged machines, local scrapworks, and makeshift furnaces that forge just enough parts, just enough weapons, just enough hope to keep the fires lit. It's a town that shouldn't work anymore. But it does.

Districts like [[STEELTOWN#Mohawk Valley|Mohawk Valley]] and [[STEELTOWN#Cannon|Cannon]] house most of the laborers, rough-and-tumble families living stacked in old brick tenements or boxcar shanties. [[STEELTOWN#Gage Park|Gage Park]] is where the kids play and the river cuts through. [[STEELTOWN#Barton|Barton]] is home to the Mayor and other more affluent citizens of [[Steeltown]]. [[STEELTOWN#The Hammer|The Hammer]] is where the hard fishermen make their living. The southern most point of [[Steeltown]], bordering the Sinner's Sea.

Officially, **[[BLUEFORCE]]** patrols these streets — [[The Duke]]’s sanctioned peacekeepers, dressed in crisp blue uniforms and stiff regulation. But the people call them **Blue Shirts**, mostly with a sneer, and they don’t hold the reins here. Not really. The real law is written in exhaust fumes and tire tracks by the **[[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]]**, a biker gang turned protectorate. Their clubhouse flies the colors over an old foundry, and their presence alone keeps most slavers, thieves, and Loyalist enforcers from pushing too hard.

There’s a balance here, but it’s a bloody one. **Mayor [[Ellis Westmount]]**, a former union-buster from [[Ashmoor]], rules like a boot on the throat, pushing The Duke’s taxes, cracking down on trade. But the [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]] doesn't take orders from city hall. Not really. They raid Loyalist convoys when the shipments get too fat, free slaves smuggled in from **Charspire**, and sometimes, if the mood's right, torch a Blue Shirt patrol car just to remind everyone who really owns these streets.

Steeltown’s people live hard, die young, and laugh anyway. They drink strong, love rough, and wear their scars like medals. They’re not waiting for liberation. They _are_ liberation, a quiet resistance of grease-stained hands and midnight deals. And if rebellion ever truly sparks across Disgraceland, it won’t begin in a palace or a chapel.

It’ll start here. In [[STEELTOWN#Gage Park|Gage Park]]. With steel, smoke, and a war cry carried on the wind.

<h2>Districts</h2>

#### Gage Park

Closest to the poisoned banks of the Rustwater River, Gage Park is where [[Steeltown]] breathes in the smoke and spits back grit. Cranes creak above rusted docks, unloading shipments by day and bodies by night. The [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]] keep their clubhouse here, casting a long shadow over the bay. South of Gage Park, the Rustwater Bay churns with sludge and secrets, a dark vein feeding the territory's lifeblood.

---

#### Barton

They call Barton the crown of Steeltown, but that crown’s cracked and copper green. It’s where Mayor [[Ellis Westmount]] lives behind reinforced glass, and the streetlamps actually work. The folk here aren't rich by [[Ashmoor]] standards, but they’ve got roofs, clean shirts, and enough distance from the riots to sleep easy. Barton pretends the rest of Steeltown doesn’t exist, and most days, it succeeds.

---

#### Mohawk Valley

The beating, bleeding heart of Steeltown. Mohawk Valley is a labyrinth of flame-belching stacks, collapsing row houses, and workers who haven’t taken a full breath in years. It’s the biggest district, packed with families who eat steel dust and shit rust. You don’t move to Mohawk. You survive it. Factory whistles mark the hours, and fists mark everything else.

---

#### Cannon

Up near Knuckles Lake, Cannon sprawls like a bad idea that never stopped growing. Steelworkers who couldn’t squeeze into [[STEELTOWN#Mohawk Valley|Mohawk Valley]] built shacks between half-finished homes and forgotten projects. Cannon’s a maze of contradictions, where kids play ball beside men with lung burns, and every decent street's got a burned-out twin. It’s loud, proud, and on the edge of boiling over.

---

#### The Hammer

Way down south, where [[Steeltown]] kisses the sea, lies The Hammer. Don’t let the pretty beaches fool you—this place works. Fishermen drag nets at dawn, while smugglers move food from [[Threshton]] under tarps and half-truths. The Hammer’s where meals are made, fish are gutted, and sea-worn families cling to the only honest trade left. It smells like salt, diesel, and fried eel.

---

#### East Mountain

Rising above the smoke and grind, East Mountain is forested, quiet, and strange. A far cry from the steel districts below, this is sacred land to followers of the [[El Santo]] faith. At its peak sits the Cathedral of [[the Grand Mascara]], white-stone and gold, visible for miles on a clear day. Pilgrims hike wooded trails while old-timers claim to see spirits in the trees. You don’t go to East Mountain to make noise. You go to listen.

---

#### West Mountain

Untamed and near-forgotten, West Mountain rolls into the wilderness like a shrug. It borders the Sinner’s Sea and stretches toward Threshton’s northern line, all rockface, deep woods, and predator trails. Nobody lives up here, not full-time, but hunters and smugglers pass through when they need space, silence, or something buried. The air’s clean. The danger’s real. It’s Steeltown’s last wild breath.
