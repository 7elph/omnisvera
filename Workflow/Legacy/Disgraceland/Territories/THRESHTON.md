# THRESHTON

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎THRESHTON
> ![[df.png|400]]  


Threshton is the dusty breadbasket of Disgraceland — a land of cracked soil, stubborn cattle, and long grudges. Stretching wide across the southern edge of the map, it’s where the grain grows, the livestock breeds, and the backbones break. The region feeds the rest of United [[Tribucia Map|Tribucia]], but barely feeds itself. Every harvest is taxed by [[The Duke]]'s regime, skimmed off by crooked ledgers and escorted out by [[Loyalist Guard#**Amberforce**|Amberforce]], the brown-shirted enforcers who watch the silos closer than they watch the streets.

At the heart of Threshton is the [[Rancher Collective]] — a loose and uneasy alliance of ranch owners who were once competitors, now forced to work together under pressure. Their ranches are sprawling compounds with weather-beaten fences and names that sound like threats: [[THRESHTON#Double Cross Ranch|Double Cross Ranch]], [[THRESHTON#Bonebreak Ranch|Bonebreak Ranch]], [[THRESHTON#Snake Kiss Ranch|Snake Kiss Ranch]], [[THRESHTON#Red Night Ranch|Red Night Ranch]], [[THRESHTON#Hangman's Ranch|Hangman’s Ranch]]. They meet under candlelight and spit into the same whiskey bottle when the deals are done. Officially, they only trade with the Duke. Unofficially, a good portion of their grain and meat finds its way into the hands of the hungry in [[Steeltown]] and [[Gutter Row]].

The region’s largest settlement is Farmington — not much more than a loose web of barns, bunkhouses, and crop mills, but it’s where the deals are struck and the rumors fly. Out here, the land is everything, and trust is rare. Outsiders aren't welcomed without purpose. Even the [[Loyalist Guard#**Amberforce**|Amberforce]] treads lightly.

Threshton’s mayor, [[Marla Rusk]], is a wiry, sun-wrinkled woman who’s survived more droughts and shootouts than most men in Disgraceland. She’s not part of the Collective, but she knows how to play them against each other when she needs to. The Duke appointed her to keep the food flowing and the workers in line — but whether she truly answers to him, or to the people of Threshton, is an open question. She smiles like a rattlesnake. Keeps a shotgun in the room during every meeting. And no one — not even the ranchers — wants to be alone with her for too long.

<h2>Districts</h2>

#### Double Cross Ranch

Westernmost and best run, **Double Cross Ranch** is [[Turner Anderson]]'s empire of dirt and precision. The docks hum with activity day and night, moving food across Tribucia like lifeblood through veins. Anderson’s operation is smooth, strict, and respected. Workers here know their roles, the land stays clean, and the books are balanced. If something fails, it gets fixed. If someone fails, they’re gone.

---

#### Red Night Ranch

[[Harp Langston]]'s legacy is carved into the soil of **Red Night Ranch**, the oldest in Tribucia. It’s where the first post-bomb harvest grew, a turning point etched into every Tribucian textbook — but here, it's religion. Named for the crimson sky the night Harp and his wife staked their claim, the ranch is sacred to some, mythic to others. The land remembers, and Harp walks it like a man guarding memory with a shotgun.

---

#### Hangman's Ranch

Won in a game of [[Sliver]] and managed like a tavern with cattle, **Hangman’s Ranch** belongs to [[Beau DuNoir]]. It hugs the border of Steeltown’s [[Steeltown#West Mountain|West Mountain]], and if the ranch itself runs well, that’s thanks to the actual ranchers. Beau's often too busy drinking, gambling, or charming someone he shouldn't. Parties at Hangman’s are legendary. So are the debts. But the cattle still come fat and the grain gets weighed — somehow.

---

#### Bonebreak Ranch

If you hear a whip crack in Threshton, it’s probably coming from **Bonebreak Ranch**. [[Freddy Blanchard]] runs it hard, runs it clean, and works the land like he's still proving something. It’s the most productive of the ranches, known across Disgraceland for its meat — thick, red, and tough like the men who raise it. Blanchard doesn’t smile much. His ranch speaks for him.

---

#### Snake Kiss Ranch

The runt of the land. **Snake Kiss Ranch** used to be a thriving estate until [[Danny Dandelion]] talked the elderly widow Greta Harp into signing it over. Since then, the place has slipped into disrepair, with fences falling and workers walking. Danny talks big, dresses louder, and pretends he’s one of the boys. He isn’t. The other ranchers know it. Snake Kiss is a joke — and Danny’s the punchline nobody laughs at.

---

#### Split Rest

Far north, where Threshton touches the Wildlands and Gravebay, sits **Split Rest** — a trading outpost turned district. Supplies, tools, weapons, smokes — it’s all here, alongside the mayor’s home and a few dusty bars that stay open too late. The winds from the bay carry secrets, and locals say the Wildlands howl at night. Split Rest is where civilization ends and something stranger begins.

---

#### Axetown

Down south in canyon country lies **Axetown**, home to the indigenous peoples who lived on this land long before the Rancher Collective ever rode through. It’s tough, dry, and carved from rock and resolve. Proud communities keep to themselves, passing down stories, fire dances, and knowledge outsiders will never understand. [[Bastian Redd]] was born here — a fighter molded from stone and spirit, raised under sun and silence.
