# CHARSPIRE

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎CHARSPIRE
> ![[chars.png|400]]  

Charspire is a choking wound in the heart of Disgraceland — a sprawling industrial hellscape belching smoke into the sky day and night. The air is thick with soot, the ground slick with oil, and the people look half-dead before they even finish their morning shift. This is where machines come to feed and men come to die. Massive factories grind endlessly through the days, pulling ore from the black guts of [[CHARSPIRE#Smashstock|Smashstock]]

The city is ruled by Mayor [[Gorren Brile]], a bloated rat of a man whose appointment came directly from **[[The Duke]]** himself. Short, round, and always sweating, **Brile** lords over the region like he’s royalty — perched high in his rust-stained tower, surrounded by sycophants and paid women. He wears a cracked monocle like it gives him class, but everyone knows he’s filth. He’s not just corrupt, he _is_ corruption. A man with no loyalty, no shame, and no bottom. If selling his own mother would buy him another month in power, he’d ask for a discount on the rope.

The streets of [[CHARSPIRE#Dog City|Dog City]] and  [[CHARSPIRE#Burnt Coast|Burnt Coast]] are a mess of flickering red lights, chainlink fences, and hunger. Workers drag their boots through blood-slick alleys to get to illegal bunkhouses, while gangs patrol the districts with homemade blades and black market guns. Brile’s enforcers, the **[[Redforce]]** — or _Red Shirts_, as the people bitterly call them — stomp through neighborhoods like gods in cheap uniforms. They don’t solve problems. They cause them. If you’re poor and bleeding, you stay that way. If you complain, you disappear.

There’s one district where the lights never go out — [[CHARSPIRE#Fistfront|Fistfront]]. Part brothel, part trap, it’s where Brile indulges most of his appetites. Drugs, girls, men, slaves, whatever. Nobody keeps count anymore. It’s a festering playground for the worst kind of men, and the only ones who rise here are the ones who’ve already given up their souls.

But even here, the fires of revolt still flicker in shadow. Whispers in the alleys. Weapons stashed beneath bunkbeds. Maps hidden under floorboards. One day, Charspire will burn — and not from the furnaces this time.

<h2>Districts</h2>

#### Gravenwild

A little less choked, a little more forgotten — Gravenwild sits on the western edge of Charspire like a cigarette burn on a silk sheet. It's rural by Charspire standards, but it hosts [[TTV Studios]], the black heart of propaganda where [[Night Time with Morris Gable]] and the [[TTV Evening News]] drip-feed the public their nightly lullabies. The fields are dry, the roads cracked, but the broadcast towers shine like teeth. Gravenwild keeps smiling, even when nobody’s watching.

---

#### Dog City

Stacked metal and spit. Dog City’s a tangled shantytown built from shipping crates, scrap, and hope that never arrived. It houses the miners and laborers of [[CHARSPIRE#Smashstock|Smashstock]], packed in shoulder-to-shoulder and drunk off fumes. Kids play in soot, women barter for water, and every fourth face’s got a scar worth hearing about. Call it what it is — a kennel for broken workers waiting to be put down.

---

#### Burnt Coast

As clean as Charspire ever gets. The Burnt Coast stretches along the Dead Waters’ edge, lined with weary trees and soot-washed skies. Mayor [[Gorren Brile]] lives here, tucked in his polished estate, away from the stink of his city. The district’s name comes from the fires that used to rage here after the [[Tetra Bomb]] fallout, but these days, it’s calm. Quiet. Dangerous in a different way.

---

#### Smashstock

This is where the air chews back. Smashstock is a sprawling industrial hell where [[Clay Industries]] runs the mines and furnaces without pause. Smoke chokes the sky, the ground trembles with drills, and the people — if you can call them that — are worked like mules. Slaves, mostly. It’s also where the [[Blackshift Massacre]] happened, when a protest was met with fire and steel. Nobody speaks of it out loud. But everyone remembers.

---

#### The Molten Zone

Northern Charspire is home to something worse than just smoke and ash. The Molten Zone is a cordoned-off wasteland, where slag fields glow under cracked earth and old structures hum with buried power. [[The Duke]]’s signs call it “Restricted.” Locals call it cursed. Rumor has it something was unearthed during a deep-core dig — not a resource, but a structure. Now RedForce patrols the perimeter in shifts, and anyone caught inside gets vaporized or vanishes. Maybe both.

---

#### Fistfront

Where [[Gutter Row]] spills into Charspire like a cut that won’t clot. Fistfront’s got the shops, food stalls, and scavenger markets that [[CHARSPIRE#Dog City|Dog City]] doesn’t. It’s where desperate folks go to trade stories for bullets, or meat for meds. The guards patrol here more often, but not out of kindness — this is the corridor where loyalty is bought, sold, and lost. Nobody smiles in Fistfront unless they’re selling something or about to steal it.

---

#### Broken Bones Bay

Named for what it gives, not what it takes. Broken Bones Bay is a jagged stretch of beach and black water, where rusted ship remains rot beside sun-bleached shanties. Kids fish with wire. Old folks drink sea-moonshine and stare out past the surf, muttering about shapes that move under the water. It’s a place for the unwanted, the forgotten, and those too stubborn to die inland. You don’t vacation here. You vanish here.
