# BODIES OF WATER  IN TRIBUCIA

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎CHARSPIRE
> ![[chars.png|400]]  

Water is both a source of survival and sorrow in Disgraceland. What little remains is poisoned, patrolled, or pulsing with death. These are the major bodies of water that shape the land, carve borders, and drown hope.

<br>

<h2>Bodies of Water</h2>

<br>

#### Sinners' Sea
- South-West edge of Tribucia  
- If the [[WATER#The Dead Waters|The Dead Waters]] are cold and silent, the **Sinner’s Sea** is loud, lawless, and cursed by name. This roiling western ocean crashes against the black cliffs of [[Steeltown#The Hammer|The Hammer]] and seeps into [[WATER#Rustwater Bay|Rustwater Bay]] and the shanty wharfs of [[GUTTER ROW#The Docks|The Docks]]. Its waves smell like salt and rust, and its tides carry in wreckage, bones, and broken cargo from lands no longer listed on maps. Old sailors swear the sea shifts. That islands once charted vanish overnight, and strange lights flicker beneath the waves. It’s said that the worst criminals — the ones even [[The Duke]] won’t keep — are exiled into its current. No records, no trials, just pushed into the surf and forgotten. Despite the danger, this sea is not empty. Smugglers, pirates, and ghost-traders sail its chop. Some say they dock on hidden coves along the western cliffs. Others claim there’s a floating market far out past where compasses spin, where you can buy anything — if you’re willing to pay in memory or blood.
- They call it the **Sinner’s Sea** because only the damned survive it.

---

#### Rustwater Bay

- Between [[Steeltown]] and [[Gutter Row]]
- A polluted bay of orange-brown runoff from the broken mills and machine-yards of [[Steeltown]]. Thick with sludge and sharp with rust stink, the bay is technically navigable — but good luck finding a boat that’ll survive more than an hour. Gulls feed on ironfish here, and even the pirates give it a wide berth.

---

#### Rustwater River

- Runs inland from [[WATER#Rustwater Bay|Rustwater Bay]]
- The toxic artery that cuts through [[Steeltown]] and spills into the gutters of [[Gutter Row]]. Locals sometimes use it for travel, and desperate scavengers paddle homemade rafts down it. Children born near it often have breathing issues. Some swear the river “talks” at night — whispering curses or laughter beneath the current.

---

#### Grave Bay

- Dead center of [[Tribucia Map|Tribucia]]
- A stagnant inland sea that once connected various trade routes. Now it sits thick and unmoving — a brackish grave for lost ferries, traitors, and political prisoners. The name isn’t poetic. Bodies float here, face-down or face-up, bloated and forgotten. On calm nights, people say the surface glows faintly red. Nobody knows why.

---

#### The Dead Waters

- The eastern coast of Tribucia.
- The **Dead Waters** stretch endlessly east of [[Charspire]] and [[Gutter Row]], swallowing the coastlines of Burnt Coast, Broken Bones Bay, and the jagged Four Tombs Islands. It is the last known ocean in this ruined world — vast, lifeless, and unnavigable by ordinary means. No merchant ships cross it. No trade lanes exist. It’s said that even the storms avoid this water. The salt here tastes bitter, wrong. Fog banks roll in without warning, and massive skeletal fish sometimes wash up on the jagged shorelines, long dead and unidentifiable. The [[Unseen Widows]] believe the sea remembers the end of the old world — that it’s where the gods once drowned themselves after the [[Tetra Bomb]]. [[The Duke]] has declared it off-limits to all but official Loyalist craft, though even they rarely venture far from the coast.
- Many say it’s not the sea that’s dead — it’s everything that enters it.

---

#### Faded Lake

- Far north, bordering [[Threshton]], [[The Drownlands]] & [[Ashmoor]]
- A frigid inland lake shrouded in pale mist most of the year. Its waters are dark, and nothing reflects on its surface. [[Coffin Island]] Prison sits like a broken tooth in the center. Rumor claims the bottom of the lake is littered with chains, bones, and drowned inmates — and that the lake itself “remembers.”  
Some locals refer to it as _The Eye That Never Shuts._

---

#### Broken Bones Bay

Named for what it gives, not what it takes. Broken Bones Bay is a jagged stretch of beach and black water, where rusted ship remains rot beside sun-bleached shanties. Kids fish with wire. Old folks drink sea-moonshine and stare out past the surf, muttering about shapes that move under the water. It’s a place for the unwanted, the forgotten, and those too stubborn to die inland. You don’t vacation here. You vanish here.
