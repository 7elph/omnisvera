# ASHMOOR

<h2>Overview</h2>

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎SOUTH OF DIAMOND VALLEY, ASHMOOR
> ![[ashmoor.png|400]]  


Ashmoor is the glittering jewel of the ruined world. The polished lie at the heart of Disgraceland. This is where [[The Duke]] sits on his rusted throne in [[The Silo]], surrounded by towers of steel and glass rising like teeth from the carcass of the old world. In the years following the [[Tetra Bomb]], Ashmoor was the first region rebuilt, and it shows. Roads are paved. Lights stay on. The air smells of expensive oil and perfumed sewage. While the rest of Tribucia struggles and rots, Ashmoor gleams with a fresh coat of power.

At the center of Ashmoor is [[The Silo]], a towering spire of metal and surveillance that houses The Duke’s command center, archives, and inner sanctum. Surrounding it are sprawling districts like [[ASHMOOR#Diamond Valley|Diamond Valley]], known for its manmade lake and private boat clubs, and [[ASHMOOR#Knifedale|Knifedale]], home of the Loyalist Guard. [[ASHMOOR#Hush Town|Hush Town]], a walled off fortress of doomsday cottages the elite of Tribucia plan to escape to in case of revolt. 

Law enforcement in Ashmoor is handled by the [[Greenforce]], a brutal, heavily equipped police unit known as the _Green Shirts_ by locals. Their armor is olive-dipped and their tactics are blunt. They do not ask questions. They do not negotiate. They are loyal to the Duke alone, and every citizen knows it. In Ashmoor, smiling wrong gets you watched. Speaking wrong gets you disappeared.

This is not a place for dreamers. It’s a city of deals, debt, and double-crosses—where power is currency and silence is survival.

<h2>Districts</h2>

#### The Silo

A gleaming rot at the heart of Ashmoor. The Silo isn’t just the name of the white-stone palace where [[The Duke]] resides, it's the name of the district built around it — a carefully manicured 11-mile zone carved out of privilege and paranoia. Security checkpoints dot the streets, and visitors walk with straight backs and quiet mouths. Grave Bay laps softly at the Silo’s edge, but the real danger is inside, where whispered orders reshape Disgraceland by the hour.

---

#### Diamond Valley

Polished to a shine no one asked for, Diamond Valley is where the power sleeps. Renovated plantation homes line the hills, each more obscene than the last. [[Jacoban Clay]] & [[Soria Langston-Clay]]. [[Flint Garritt]]. [[Warren Scottyard]]. These are the names carved into the gates, each estate fenced and flanked by private guards. The streets are smooth, the lawns trimmed, and the silence enforced. Diamond Valley doesn’t just house the elite — it hides them.

---

#### Amber Valley

Just a little further out from the rotunda glamour of Diamond Valley lies Amber, the quieter cousin with a matching bloodline. The properties here are still large, still clean, but the fences are lower and the names on the mailboxes carry a little less weight. Rising stars, minor Loyalist officials, and ambitious merchants make their homes here, hoping one day to be invited uphill. It’s all glass, stucco, and ambition.

---

#### Homa Point

Named for Homa Bahram, the explorer who never made it back, Homa Point juts out over rocky cliffs and stares down the Wildlands with stubborn defiance. The district carries a kind of haunted reverence, more memorial than neighborhood. There are some settlements here — modest cabins, weather posts, and ranger stations — but most folks avoid it. The wind screams at night, and some say it carries Homa’s last breath.

---

#### Greencove

Greencove is a rare pocket of quiet in Ashmoor — a northern stretch of forest bordering the eerie Faded Lake. It’s mostly used by wealthy hunters, off-duty officers, and those who need to disappear for a few days. Old wood lodges creak beside still waters, and mist curls through the trees like it’s listening. Locals say the lake whispers at dawn. Nobody’s tried to prove it.

---

#### Hush Town

A fortress disguised as a retreat. Hush Town is a walled district pressed up against Faded Lake and brushing the edge of [[The Drownlands]]. Behind its reinforced gates lie massive doomsday cottages — panic mansions, really — built for the elite in case Ashmoor burns. Bunkers, satellite dishes, emergency stores. It’s where the powerful plan to wait out the end of the world. The place is armed to the teeth, but they still call it a “getaway.”

---

#### Knifedale

Knifedale cuts through the territory like its name implies. Home to the main Loyalist Guard training compound and the largest base in Ashmoor, it’s a district of barracks, kill-houses, and saluting. New recruits bleed on the gravel here while high-ranking officers bark from polished steps. Most civilians aren’t allowed in, but the ones who live nearby have learned to sleep through gunfire and marching boots.
