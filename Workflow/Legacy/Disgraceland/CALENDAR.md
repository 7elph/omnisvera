# 📜 Disgraceland Holidays & Events

---

## 🧊 1st of Frostsun — New Year's Day

**Type:** Survival Celebration  
**Meaning:** Marks the first day of the new year. Unlike pre-fall celebrations, Disgraceland's New Year is grim — a hard-earned survival marker, not a party.  
**Common Practices:** Fires are lit. Families gather quietly if they can. Simple meals are shared. Thanks are given for surviving another brutal year.  
**Cultural Notes:** In poor territories, it's seen as a somber day. In [[Ashmoor]], elites sometimes hold small balls or ceremonies.

---

## 🥊 15th–20th of Rainsun (Every 4 Years) — Noche de Lucha

**Type:** Fighting Tournament  celebrating [[El Santo]]
**Meaning:** A brutal one night, 16 man, single elimination tournament where champions from across Disgraceland battle for the title of **Grand Luchador**.  A festival celebrating [[El Santo]] takes place in the days before and after.
**Common Practices:** Street festivals, underground gambling, regional pride matches.  
**Cultural Notes:** Winning [[Noche de Lucha]] brings instant fame, wealth, and influence. It's the single most-watched event in Disgraceland, broadcast even into the poorest zones.

---

## 🏛️ 14th of Bloomsun — Ashmoor Festival

**Type:** Propaganda Celebration  
**Meaning:** Celebrates the official founding and rebuilding of [[Ashmoor]] by [[The Duke]].  
**Common Practices:** Parades, concerts, speeches praising The Duke and Ashmoor’s prosperity.  
**Cultural Notes:** Mostly celebrated by the rich and loyal. Poorer citizens in other regions see it as a sick joke.

---

## ⚫ 23rd of Searsun — Blackshift Massacre Memorial Day

**Type:** Day of Mourning  
**Meaning:** Remembrance of the workers and miners slaughtered by [[Final Force]] during the [[Blackshift Massacre]].  
**Common Practices:** Silent marches. Black flags hung over doors. Work stoppages (illegal but widespread).  
**Cultural Notes:** Especially honored in [[Charspire]] and [[Steeltown]]. [[Ashmoor]] officials pretend to recognize it but barely mention it.

---

## ☢️ 4th of Ambertide — Anniversary of the Tetra Bomb

**Type:** Global Remembrance  
**Meaning:** Marks the catastrophic detonation of the [[Tetra Bomb]], which ended the [[Water Wars]] but shattered the world.  
**Common Practices:** Bells toll at noon. Some regions observe full-day fasts. Others light black candles in memory.  
**Cultural Notes:** Bitterness, resentment, and anti-Duke sentiment runs high on this day. Guard patrols are quietly doubled in vulnerable cities.

---

## 🌾 30th of Ambertide — Harvest Festival

**Type:** Survival Feast  
**Meaning:** Final harvest feast before The Withering season begins. Families and communities gather to celebrate the earth’s last bounty.  
**Common Practices:** Large feasts. Marriages and pacts often made. Debts are expected to be settled before sundown.  
**Cultural Notes:** If harvests are bad, tensions and violence can break out during or after the festival.

---

## ⚒️ 18th of Hollowtide — Worker's Day (Steeltown & Charspire Only)

**Type:** Labor Honor Day  
**Meaning:** Celebrates the contributions — and exploitation — of laborers who keep Disgraceland functioning.  
**Common Practices:** Speeches, rallies, strikes (often illegal).  
**Cultural Notes:** Only officially recognized in [[Steeltown]] and [[Charspire]]. Heavily monitored. Arrests and disappearances spike after rallies.

---

## 🌑 30th of Hollowtide — Dark Night

**Type:** Forced Anarchy Observance  
**Meaning:** Marks the anniversary of a failed rebellion against [[The Duke]]. As punishment and reminder, all [[Loyalist Guard]]s are ordered to stand down for one night.  
**Common Practices:** Murders, looting, revenge killings, liberations. Citizens barricade themselves inside or participate in the chaos.  
**Cultural Notes:** [[Ashmoor]] remains protected by [[Final Force]]. Everywhere else becomes lawless.  
**Quote:** > "One night of freedom. One lifetime of chains."

---

## 🔥 20th of Whitetide — Day of the Ember Saint

**Type:** Religious Holiday  
**Meaning:** Honoring the sacrifice and heroism of the Ember Saint — the savior who braved fire to rescue survivors long ago.  
**Common Practices:** Fire ceremonies, candle lighting, storytelling.  
**Cultural Notes:** Especially sacred to the [[Church of the Ember Saint]]. Even nonbelievers often quietly observe the rituals in the dead of winter.
