# Galliah, the Stillborn Star

> [!column] Information
>> [!rustwater] Description
Galliah is the ancient dead sun, the stillborn star that slumbers beyond mortal sight. To the faithful of the [[Word of the Stillborn Star]], Galliah is not simply a celestial body — it is a god, a grave, and a prophecy.
Once a star that sought to burn but failed, Galliah cracked under its own sorrow, bleeding faint silver light into the abyss. In the shattered legends of pre-Tetra times, it is said Galliah **"tried to awaken but was denied the flame"** — an eternal symbol of futility, cycles, and the inevitable rebirth of worlds from ruin.
Its broken corpse now drifts at the edge of all things, encased in tendrils of ghostly mist, orbited by debris from civilizations long forgotten.

> "We are the ash of its dreams. We are the silence of its breath."

## Appearance

- **Shape**: A vast, blackened sphere, like volcanic glass shattered by unseen hands.
    
- **Surface**: Crisscrossed by deep fractures; faint, ghostly silver light leaks from the cracks.
    
- **Atmosphere**: Ashen mist swirls around it, remnants of ancient failed solar flares.
    
- **Orbit**: A graveyard of broken asteroids, ruins of shattered worlds, drifting slowly around it.
    

---

## Beliefs Surrounding Galliah

- **The Silent Cycle**: Followers believe all things exist in a cycle of creation, collapse, and rebirth — Galliah’s failure is not an end, but a silent waiting.
    
- **The Awakening**: One day, Galliah will stir, not with light, but with **perfect silence**, heralding the final collapse of the broken world.
    
- **The Stillborn Gospel**: Ancient priests like Mesh'den recorded visions of Galliah's dreams — barren worlds, forgotten empires, and endless night — and taught that humanity must embrace futility to achieve true peace.
    
- **Ash Children**: Some extremist sects of Stillborns mutilate their bodies with brands and ash to "mark themselves for Galliah" and welcome the collapse early.
    

---

## Influence on Disgraceland

- **The [[Word of the Stillborn Star]]** is the oldest faith in the world.
    
- Symbols of Galliah — a cracked black circle leaking silver threads — are still found carved into ruins, graveyards, and hidden shrines across United Tribucia.
    
- People praise Galliah when things go right, and curse the star god when things go wrong.

---

## Rumors and Legends

- **The Shard of Galliah**: Whispers tell of a fragment of Galliah that fell to Earth before recorded history. Some claim it slumbers deep in [[the Drownlands]], poisoning the swamps and feeding ancient gods.
    
- **The Dead Choir**: Some travelers claim to hear ghostly singing when staring into the night sky near the Dead Waters — a cosmic lament attributed to Galliah’s ancient dreamers.
    
- **The Hollow Crown**: According to old tales, a king once forged a crown from a piece of Galliah’s fallen ash. He ruled a silent kingdom where nobody spoke and all eventually vanished without a trace.
