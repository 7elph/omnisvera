# 📺 CRT Televisions

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎Ashmoor Festival
> ![[crt.png|500]]  

Issued by decree of [[The Duke]], every family in Tribucia receives **one government-supplied CRT television** — whether they want it or not. These bulky, crackling boxes are considered “essential technology” by the regime, intended to connect the people with **[[TTV Studios|TTV]]**, the official state broadcast network.

---

## 📡 Broadcast Content

The only available channel on every set is **TTV**. There are no remotes, no buttons to change the channel. The airwaves are strictly monitored and centrally controlled from [[TTV Studios]] in [[Charspire]].

**TTV Programming Includes:**
- 🛑 **The [[State of Tribucia]]** – Weekly broadcast from [[The Duke]] himself.
- 🌙 **[[Night Time with Morris Gable]]** – Late-night propaganda and entertainment, Mon–Thurs.
- 🥊 **[[WLL: Fight Night on TTV]]** – Broadcast live every Restday (Friday), sponsored by [[Flint Garritt]].
- 📰 **TTV Nightly News** – A state-run news program (anchor TBD).

**When no scheduled programming is on-air, the screen defaults to eerie static.** There is no off switch — only unplugging the device or smashing it, the latter of which is strictly prohibited by law.

---

## 🏚️ Use Among the Poor

In the decaying slums of [[Gutter Row]], the smokestacks of [[Charspire]], and the shantytowns of [[Steeltown]], the glowing CRT screens serve a second purpose: **light source**. Residents often cluster around the flickering blue-white static in otherwise pitch-black homes. Some even time their evenings by the hum of the screen.

The televisions are seen as both a curse and a convenience — constant propaganda machines that also keep the shadows at bay.

---

## 🕵️‍♂️ Rumors & Urban Myths

Despite the controlled nature of TTV, whispers circulate that **some televisions have displayed... something else**. Unknown images. Strange figures. Messages not approved by The Duke.

No one has ever officially confirmed these sightings, but:
- A boy in [[Steeltown]] claimed he saw **a woman in a mask whispering names**.
- A miner in [[Charspire]] swears the screen turned blood red for six straight minutes.
- A vendor in [[Gutter Row]] insists he saw **[[Ora Razchek]]’s death house** appear in the static for a brief moment.

The Loyalist Guard has dismissed all such claims as "hallucinations caused by fatigue or substance abuse."

---

## 🧠 Trivia

- Most units are decades old, scavenged and rebuilt by [[Clay Millwrights]].
- Tampering with the TV is punishable by **20 lashes or 5 years of labor**, per Duke Mandate #47.
- A hidden chip inside each unit logs viewing duration and reports it back to TTV HQ.
