# Backburn Shows

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ Backburn Show
> ![[bbshow.png|450]]  

## Overview  
**Backburn Shows** or Backburners are informal, often spontaneous music gatherings that erupt across Disgraceland — in alleyways, ruins, scrapyards, and fields. Named for the way they *"burn backward"* through neighborhoods like wildfire, these gatherings start as small jams and quickly swell into full-blown, pulse-throbbing performances under rusted scaffolding or broken moonlight.  

Though technically illegal under United Tribucian law for *“encouraging non-sanctioned assembly and the performance of subversive lyrics,”* Backburns are sacred to the common folk. Here, no stage, no tickets, no rules — just sound and sweat and something that almost feels like freedom.

---

## Origins  
The term *Backburn* was first coined by early street poets in [[Steeltown]] who described the way word of a jam would spread backward through the districts, lighting up ears long before boots hit gravel. These fires of rhythm couldn’t be contained.  

Legends say both [[Dusty Dillon]] and [[Jaden Kade]] first turned heads during midnight Backburns — one behind a collapsed factory furnace, the other in the shell of a bombed-out theater in [[Charspire]]. Their sounds — gritty, defiant, unfiltered — became the anthems of the ignored.

---

## Common Locations
- **[[Steeltown]]**: Often in rusted-out loading bays, rooftops, or inside gutted rail cars.  
- **[[Gutter Row]]**: Hidden courtyards off the [[Three Nail Market]], where drums and synths echo through poison-lit alleys.  
- **[[Charspire]]**: In old mine shafts, scorched lots, or beneath scaffolds during [[Loyalist Guard#Redforce|Redforce]] shift changes.  
- **[[The Drownlands]]**: Rare, but when they happen — eerie, swamp-lit, and whispered about for months.  
- **[[Threshton]]**: Usually tied to secret Rancher celebrations, shielded by crops and prairie silence.

---

## Enforcement & Rebellion  
[[TTV Studios]] and the [[Loyalist Guard]] officially denounce Backburn Shows as breeding grounds for *“anti-Tribucian messaging and sedition.”* Patrols are dispatched to break them up, but the gatherings shift fast — and locals protect them like sacred rites.  

Whispers claim even some lower-ranking Loyalists attend in secret, pulling caps low and mouthing lyrics with fire in their throats.

---

## Cultural Role  
Backburns aren’t just about music. They’re grief rituals. Love letters. Acts of war. Here, banned instruments wail, forbidden words fly, and hope lives for three chords longer than it should.  

Some say if you listen hard enough, you’ll hear the future humming through the static.
