# 🥃 Swillpots of Disgraceland

Swillpots are the lifeblood of the broken people of Disgraceland — rough, loud, chaotic watering holes where drinks flow, fists fly, and secrets slip like spilled liquor on a crooked counter. While technically unsanctioned, they’ve become so culturally ingrained that even [[The Duke]] dares not outlaw them, despite his well-known hatred for alcohol. He’s called it “the rust in the gears of rebuilding,” but even his most Loyalist advisors know that banning swill would spark instant riots.

### 📜 History

The first Swillpot was nothing more than a hole-in-the-wall lean-to near the original strip mine in [[Charspire]], almost 50 years ago. Miners needed somewhere to forget how close they were to death every day, and a one-eyed ex-barkeep named [[Renny Slub]] gave them that place. His original shack, **The First Spill**, still stands — though it’s mostly a museum piece now, kept up by locals out of spite and pride.

From there, Swillpots spread across Tribucia, becoming a sacred refuge for the overworked, the outcast, the outlaw, and the brokenhearted.

---

## 🧃 Standard Fare

**Popular Drinks:**
- Lager
- Ale
- Whiskey
- Rum
- Wine

Most Swillpots also serve greasy food: meats, stews, thick breads, or salt-fried root vegetables. The rare ones offer fancier mixed drinks or imports — but expect to pay heavy in **slugs**.

## 🍻 Notable Swillpots by Region

### **STEELTOWN – The Furnace Gullet**
A loud, sweat-soaked pit of hammered steel and busted stools. Locals say fights here settle quicker than court disputes. Always hot inside, no matter the season. Home turf for a few off-duty Wrecking Crew bruisers.

### **ASHMOOR – The Amber Touch**
A 'high-class' Swillpot by Disgraceland standards. Velvet seats, expensive wines, and waitstaff that’ll rob you blind with a smile. Frequented by minor bureaucrats, spies, and smugglers in nice coats.

### **GUTTER ROW – The Slick Room**
Run out of the bottom floor of a collapsing tenement in Vileby. Walls drip from busted pipes, and rats are considered regulars. Nobody knows who actually owns it, but everyone pays the same: don’t cause trouble, or you disappear.

### **CHARSPIRE – The Spill House**
The original. Old, blackened stone walls with rusted beams overhead. Hasn’t cleaned its tankards in years. RedForce guards have an unspoken rule to leave it alone — even they need to forget sometimes.

### **THRESHTON – The Broken Bale**
A rancher-run hideaway where cowboys drink their fill and trade secrets over sour mash and molasses bread. Anyone badmouthing the [[Rancher Collective]] gets their boots nailed to the porch.

## 🧨 Notes

- The Duke would outlaw alcohol entirely if he believed he could get away with it.
- Instead, he taxes it heavily in Ashmoor, ignores it in Steeltown, and pretends it doesn't exist in Gutter Row.
- Despite his hatred, it’s common knowledge that some of his most loyal guards drink off-duty.
