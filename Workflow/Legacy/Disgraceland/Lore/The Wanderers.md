## 🎤 Overview  
**The Wanderers** are a post-collapse rock group formed in the streets of Charspire, now known across [[DISGRACELAND]] as the nightly house band on *Night Time with Morris Gable*. While the regime promotes them as “a symbol of restored culture,” many citizens — especially the young and restless — feel something deeper pulsing beneath the music.

Led by [[Jaden Kade]], a wiry frontman with a scorched voice and rebel heart, The Wanderers walk a dangerous line between performance and resistance. Their lyrics ride the edge of censorship, and every show feels like a gamble — how much truth can be hidden in a song before someone notices?

---

## 🎶 Style & Themes  
Their sound is a fusion of post-industrial blues, rustrock, and stripped-down synthwave.  
- Instruments are salvaged, wired by hand, often shorting out mid-performance.  
- Lyrics often reference roads, fire, static, chains, and lost memory — metaphors for rebellion, surveillance, and grief.

**Notable Songs:**  
- *“Last Light Left”* — Believed to be about the failed uprising in [[Steeltown]]  
- *“Cracked Signal”* — Features a reversed vocal loop that some claim is a coded message  
- *“Rustwater Hymn”* — Banned from public radio three weeks after debut  
- *“King of Glass”* — Thought to be a veiled insult toward [[The Duke]]  
- *“Deadman Waltz”* — A fan favorite known to stir emotion in live crowds

---

## 🎤 Relationship with Morris Gable  
Their inclusion on *Night Time with Morris Gable* is widely seen as strategic — both for the regime, which uses their popularity to retain viewer interest, and for Jaden Kade, who uses the airtime to echo unrest through rhythm.

**Publicly:**  
- Morris praises them weekly as “the soul of the nation.”  
- The band appears polite and cooperative — but never speaks much during interviews.

**Privately:**  
- Jaden Kade despises Morris but respects the opportunity.  
- It’s a cold truce: ratings in exchange for silence.

---

## 🔥 Rebellion Rumors  
- Multiple resistance cells have reportedly used The Wanderers’ lyrics as code phrases.  
- Bootlegged live recordings circulate in outlaw zones.  
- It’s believed the band helps fund small acts of sabotage using their TTV stipend.  
- Some fans claim to have received blank tapes from the group containing anti-regime broadcasts when played backward.
