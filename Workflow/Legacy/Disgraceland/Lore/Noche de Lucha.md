# NOCHE DE LUCHA

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ Backburn Show
> ![[ancientnl.png|450]]  

## Overview
**Noche de Lucha (“Night of the Fight”) is a one night, 16 person single elimination tournament that takes place every four years.** 
**Open to All:**   Traditionally reserved for faithful of [[El Santo]], the tournament is now open to all, regardless of faith, in recognition of the world’s upheaval and the need for unity. Even an Ember Saint won the last Noche de Lucha. An event talked about in every corner of Disgraceland.
 **Selection:**  The Senior Enmascarados—the highest council of the faith—select 16 fighters based on merit, reputation, and sometimes, rumor or legend or popular support.
**The Arena:**  Held in the Arena Mascarada in [[STEELTOWN#East Mountain|East Mountain]], [[Steeltown]].
**The Celebration:**  Noche de Lucha is a festival as much as a tournament—food, music, street games, and weeks of communal joy and rivalry.
**The Prize:**  The winner is crowned Grand Luchador, head of the faith and symbol of the era’s spirit for the next four years.
**Enduring Tradition:**  War, bombs, famine—nothing has stopped Noche de Lucha. It is the one promise the world has kept.

## ANCIENT ORIGINS

Since time immemorial, every four years on the 15th–20th of [[Calendar|Rainsun]], Noche de Lucha has been held.

- **Sacred Combat:** The earliest Nights took place at the center of ancient villages, on stone platforms beneath torchlight. Masked warriors fought not for sport, but to resolve disputes, seek the blessing of their gods, or determine leaders.
- **The Masked Champion:** Legends tell of a figure known only as **[[El Santo]]**, the first to don a sacred mask and win the Night—not through brute strength, but through heart, cunning, and mercy.
- **The Eternal Ring:** These battles happened in rings drawn in dirt, carved in stone, or built from woven ropes—always as a circle, representing eternity and unity.
- **Rituals & Myths:** Every Noche began with offerings, music, and storytelling. Winners were believed to carry the favor of the divine, and sometimes, to become living saints.

## EVOLUTION

The tradition endured. As civilizations rose and fell, the Night was passed down through generations, its true origins lost but its rituals surviving.  
Later cultures reshaped it—sometimes as gladiator games, sometimes as wrestling, sometimes as rebellion against tyrants.  
But always:  
- Masks, to honor the unknown gods and spirits  
- A ring, to contain the struggle  
- The winner’s right to challenge fate and change their life

## LEGACY IN DISGRACELAND

In the world of Disgraceland, **Noche de Lucha** is the oldest surviving ritual. It is the **heartbeat of the Faith of El Santo** and the unbroken thread connecting the present to a forgotten age.

Even after the fall, the bombs, and the end of empires, the Night remains.  
Each four-year cycle is not just a contest—but a reenactment of a myth older than memory.

## CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

**Noche de Lucha** is:
- The living memory of ancient peoples
- Proof that unity, struggle, and hope endure
- A bridge between mortals and the divine
