## History

> [!infobox]
>
> ![[wll.png|260]]

- **Origins:**  
  Lucha Libre grew from the old [[Noche de Lucha]] tournaments—an ancient tradition of combat, now evolved into a spectator sport for a broken world.
- **Golden Age:**  
  Before the [[Tetra Bomb]], the **WLL (World Lucha Libre)** was a global phenomenon. Champions toured the world, and the best fighters became household names.
- **The Fall:**  
  After the [[Tetra Bomb]], the sport vanished. Only small, dangerous underground matches survived in the chaos.
- **Revival:**  
  In the last 15 years, as Disgraceland clawed back to stability, a wealthy industrialist (a bitter rival to [[Jacoban Clay]]) resurrected the WLL—building a new arena in [[Gutter Row]] and sparing no expense on belts, hype, and spectacle.  
  Anyone with fists, dreams, or something to prove can enter—win, and you might become a legend.

---

## Culture & Masks

- **Masks:**  
  In modern Lucha Libre, masks are *not* required. The mask remains a sacred symbol for the clergy and the most faithful of [[El Santo]], but the sport itself is about the fighter’s reputation, skill, and swagger.
- **The Show:**  
  Theatrics, promos, and hype are a must. Fighters adopt bold personas, enter the arena like legends, and stir the crowd before a punch is even thrown.
- **The Fights:**  
  All-out MMA—no scripts, no predetermined winners, no mercy.  
  Fighters are judged by toughness, technique, and the ability to thrill a crowd.

---

## The Modern WLL

- **Arena:**  
  The new WLL arena in [[Gutter Row]] is part fortress, part theater—a beacon of light (and vice) in the chaos.
- **Fighter Recruitment:**  
  Open call. The desperate, the daring, and the washed-up all get a shot. The best rise fast; the weak go home broken or worse.
- **Titles:**  
  Real belts, real champions. Winning one means respect, money, and all the trouble that comes with fame in Disgraceland.
- **Promoters:**  
  The WLL’s owner is a kingmaker—pitting would-be legends against each other and using the league to grow his own influence.
- **Entertainment:**  
  Beyond the fights, every show is an event: music, fireworks, dancing, wild costumes. It’s part bloodsport, part circus, all survival.

---

## Impact on Society

- **Unity & Escape:**  
  For a few hours, faith, faction, and class mean nothing. People come together to watch the fights, forget their troubles, and gamble their last coins.
- **Fame & Fortune:**  
  Fighters can become folk heroes—or cautionary tales.  
  Some win riches, others disappear after a brutal loss.
- **Rivalries:**  
  The WLL is a pressure valve for Disgraceland. Old grudges and street feuds often play out in the cage, with the crowd roaring for blood.

## Famous Champions (Examples)

- **“The Sand Viper”** – undefeated, merciless, beloved by the crowd, but feared in the locker room.
- **Freya “The Iron Widow”** – former Unseen Widow who shocked the world with her fighting spirit.
- **Marcus “Steeljaw” Torres** – a Steeltown legend known for never tapping out.

## Notable Moments

- **The Arena Riot (Year 2182):**  
  When a match ended in a disputed stoppage, fans stormed the cage. The promoter doubled security after.
- **The First Open Challenge:**  
  The owner’s “anyone can fight” policy led to a month where a farmer, a priest, and a disgraced Loyalist all stepped into the ring—none won, but the city still talks about it.

---

## Connections to Faith

- **El Santo’s Influence:**  
  The faith’s values—endurance, honor, and justice—live on in the sport’s code, even if most fighters now go unmasked.
- **Noche de Lucha:**  
  Once every four years, the line between faith and sport blurs as champions from the WLL are invited to compete in the greatest tournament of all.
