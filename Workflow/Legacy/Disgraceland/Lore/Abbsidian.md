## The Black Pit

**Abbsidian** is the word whispered across Disgraceland to describe the after-realm where damned souls are cast. It is the Disgraceland version of Hell, the underworld, and eternal torment. Whether spoken by merchants in [[Vileby]], drunks in the [[Swillpots]], or frightened slaves in the [[Three Nail Market]], the meaning remains the same: Abbsidian is where the cursed go when death itself shows mercy.

To fall into Abbsidian is not simply to die. It is to vanish from existence, to be swallowed by a pit of endless black where light, sound, and memory fade into nothing. The condemned are said to scream for centuries before their voices are finally devoured by the void.

---

## Ancient Folklore

The true origin of the word _Abbsidian_ is unknown. It predates the Water Wars by thousands of years, carried by the earliest survivors who crossed into what would become Disgraceland. The term may have roots in the forgotten worship of ancient gods.

One ancient legend ties Abbsidian directly to the betrayal of **Vellokar**, the god once worshipped by old-world exiles. When Vellokar was bound beneath the earth, his throne became Abbsidian. His daughter, **[[Valkaara]]**, swore to drag all oathbreakers, betrayers, and murderers into that pit to join her father’s endless suffering.

## Beliefs and Superstitions

- Many in [[Gutter Row]] believe that when the **Pale Girl** (Hella) takes victims, she delivers them to Abbsidian.
    
- The [[Unseen Widows]] refer to Abbsidian as _The Black Below_, and warn never to speak its name under the light of a dying moon.
    
- The Wrecking Crew have an old phrase: _"The road ends at Abbsidian."_
    

---

## Common Sayings

- “Abbsidian waits for liars.”
    
- “Even the dead fear Abbsidian.”
    
- “She’ll drag you to the Black Pit.”
    
- “The soul screams longest in Abbsidian.”
    

---

## Modern Usage

The term has become so ingrained in Disgraceland that _Abbsidian_ is now slang used in both criminal and Loyalist circles.

- Blackforce officers warn prisoners: _"Better start talking, or I’ll send you to Abbsidian myself."_
    
- Gang enforcers threaten debtors with _“a fast drop into Abbsidian.”_
    
- Some desperate prisoners wear makeshift charms etched with crude symbols to ward off the pull of Abbsidian.
