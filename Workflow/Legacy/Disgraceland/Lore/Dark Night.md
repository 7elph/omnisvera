#  Dark Night


## Overview

> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎DARK NIGHT
> ![[dn.png|400]]  

**Date:** 30th of Hollowtide  
**Type:** Forced Observance / Day of Lawlessness
- Dark Night is a relatively new event in Disgraceland’s troubled history.  
- It began after a massive but failed uprising against **The Duke** — an event that shook his regime but ultimately ended in brutal suppression.
- Rather than simply erase the memory, The Duke twisted it into an annual reminder of his absolute power.  
- Every year since the uprising, on the 30th of [[CALENDAR|Hollowtide]], **The Duke orders all Loyalist Guard forces to stand down** for exactly 24 hours across all territories.
- **Only the Final Force remains active**, stationed around **The Silo** in Ashmoor to protect the wealthy and the ruling elite.

## Modern Observance

- **No Guard patrols** exist during Dark Night across [[Steeltown]], [[Charspire]], [[Gutter Row]], [[Threshton]], and [[the Drownlands]].
- **Ashmoor**, by contrast, becomes a fortress — [[Final Force]] soldiers patrol heavily around [[The Silo]] and its surrounding elite districts.
- **Crime explodes** across the unprotected regions: murders, robberies, fires, uprisings, and revenge killings surge.
- Some gangs and factions **plan entire operations** around Dark Night, knowing there will be no government interference.
- Many ordinary citizens **barricade their homes** and pray to survive until sunrise.

Meanwhile, within [[Ashmoor]], the night takes on a disturbingly festive tone.  
At **The Silo**, the wealthy and politically connected hold **grand parties** — drinking, dancing, and celebrating their untouchable status while the world outside burns.

---

## Cultural Significance

- **The Duke's Message:**  
  > "You are beasts without my rule. You will tear yourselves apart without my leash."
- **Psychological Warfare:**  
  Dark Night reinforces fear, dependency, and hopelessness. The chaos keeps citizens divided and desperate.
- **Resistance Symbol:**  
  For rebels and radicals, Dark Night is a solemn reminder of the original uprising — and a yearly call to remember those who fought and died for freedom.

> "In Ashmoor, they toast. In Steeltown, they bleed."


# 🧠 Notes

- Some underground movements now see Dark Night not as a defeat, but as an opportunity for resistance operations and assassinations.
- Religious cults tied to the **[[Word of the Stillborn Star]]** or **[[Faith of Mirella]]** hold secret rites during Dark Night, believing the veil between worlds is thinnest on this night.
