# THE BLACKSHIFT MASSACRE

> [!column] Information
>> [!rustwater] Description
> **Date:** **2165** (21 years ago)
> **Location:** Blackshift Pit Mine, [[Charspire]]
> **Summary:** The **Blackshift Massacre** marks the most infamous atrocity committed by the [[Final Force]]. What began as a workers’ rebellion over deadly conditions and starvation rations in the Blackshift Pit ended in blood and infamy.  
[[The Duke]] ordered the FF to the site, seeking to end the standoff. He never gave a direct kill order, but the FF’s then-commander (now deceased) chose brute force.
> 
>> [!rustwater] Final Force
>> ![[bs.png|400]]

## The Massacre  
- **FF troopers**—including a young [[Sgt. Rottwyler]]—stormed the mine.
- All workers—men, women, and children—were massacred.  
- Survivors and witnesses claim the FF unleashed their arsenal without warning or mercy.
- The ground ran red and the smoke from burning equipment choked [[Charspire]] for days.

## Aftermath  
- [[The Duke]]’s silence and slow public response fueled outrage across Disgraceland.
- Only after mounting public fury did the regime admit the “grave mistake.” The families were all paid to keep quiet.
- The original FF commander died mysteriously within months; some say as penance, others say as a cover-up.

## The Reporter  
- A young, unnamed reporter for the **Tribucia Tribute** was on-site—hidden, taking photos.
- Their images, smuggled out and widely copied, became iconic symbols of state brutality.
- The reporter’s work fueled dissent and memorialized the massacre for future generations.

## Raynar Ventura’s Role  
- [[Raynar Ventura]] entered the Duke’s orbit soon after the Blackshift Massacre, seizing the opportunity to ingratiate himself during the Duke’s lowest moment.
- Ventura used his political cunning to help reshape the Duke’s public image, controlling the narrative and managing fallout in the aftermath.

## Legacy  
- “Blackshift” is now a curse in [[Charspire]] and across Disgraceland.
- Annual vigils and underground art keep the memory alive.
- The massacre’s infamy permanently stained the Duke’s rule; whispers about what happened that day still spread.
- Many see this as the moment the public learned the true cost of the Duke’s iron rule—and the birth of widespread dissent.
