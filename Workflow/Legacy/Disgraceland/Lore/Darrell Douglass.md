## Overview  
**Darrell Douglass** was the father of [[Jimmy Douglass]] and [[Mark Jeffrey]], and one of the founding members of the original [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]]. A former steelworker turned revolutionary, Darrell was a symbol of defiance in the early days of Disgraceland’s formation — known for his booming voice, unbreakable work ethic, and fierce loyalty to the people of [[Steeltown]].

He was executed on the direct orders of [[The Duke]] nearly two decades ago, after refusing to cooperate with [[Ashmoor]]’s new food and fuel directives. His death marked a turning point in [[Steeltown]]’s relationship with the regime — a martyrdom that galvanized the rise of the modern [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]].

In the early years of the reconstruction, Darrell Douglass formed a quiet but powerful alliance with [[Harp Langston]], the hard-edged rancher from [[Threshton]]. Though they came from different worlds — one a union man of steel and soot, the other a farmer forged from soil and sweat — they saw eye to eye on the most important thing: the people had been forgotten. When Darrell restarted the first mill in [[Steeltown]] in 2151, Harp rode north to see it with his own eyes. That meeting sparked the start of an unspoken brotherhood. Harp sent food. Darrell sent tools. Together, they created the Black Route — a secret lifeline of grain and supplies flowing between Steeltown and the southern fields.

But like all quiet revolutions, it didn’t stay hidden. In 2158, The Duke’s men cracked down. Darrell was captured and forced into steel production. Harp wanted to act — to fight, to break him out — but the newly forming Rancher Collective refused. The risk to Threshton was too great. Darrell and Harp argued for the last time, and their bond fractured. For the next decade, Darrell was a ghost — kept alive only to fuel the machine he once tried to stop. When he was finally hanged in 2167, Harp didn’t attend. But word spread that a grain silo burned that night in Threshton, ash scorched into the dirt in the shape of a hammer and sickle. The message was clear. Their friendship may have ended in silence, but the respect never died.

## Known For  
- Organizing the first major work strikes after the [[Water Wars]]  
- Personally leading the blockade of [[Steeltown]]’s railyards  
- Declaring [[The Duke]] a “parasite in a crown” in a broadcasted protest  
- Being publicly hanged under [[Loyalist guard]] surveillance outside the old steel gates  

---

## Legacy  
- His sons, [[Jimmy Douglass]] and [[Mark Jeffrey]], both inherited his drive but took different paths.  
  - Jimmy became  the hammer — carrying his father’s fire and fury forward.  
  - Mark became the heart — trying to keep the Wrecking Crew honorable.
- Steeltown still marks the anniversary of his death with a midnight vigil known as *The Burn Mark*, where torches are lit along the old railyard fences.  
- A faded mural of Darrell still stands on a warehouse wall — chipped, bullet-riddled, but never painted over.
