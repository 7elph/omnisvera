# 📡 [[State of Tribucia]]
> “*Your land. My vision. Our duty.*” — *[[The Duke]]*

## 🗓 Schedule
- **Every [[CALENDAR|Alphaday]]** at **20:00hrs**  
- **Broadcast live from** [[The Silo]] on **[[TTV Studios]]**  
- **Mandatory viewing** across [[TRIBUCIA MAP]], though enforcement is unofficial and widely ignored outside of [[Ashmoor]].

---

## 🎙 Overview
The **State of Tribucia** is a weekly broadcast led by **[[The Duke]]**, designed to **project authority, maintain illusionary order, and reinforce loyalty** across the fractured zones of Disgraceland. Though framed as a patriotic update, each address is carefully crafted propaganda — blending truth with distortion, veiled threats with hollow optimism.

Each episode opens with a militaristic version of the *Hymn of Unity*, followed by a wide shot of **[[The Duke]]** standing beneath a giant eagle-and-scythe emblem in a cold metal chamber deep inside [[The Silo]]. He speaks alone — no advisors, no questions, just the voice of a ruler to his subjects.

The broadcasts vary in length from **20 minutes to an hour**, depending on current events, [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew|Wrecking Crew]] movements, political unrest, or how much ego-stroking **[[The Duke]]** needs that week.

---

## 🗝 Purpose
- Reaffirm **The Duke’s control and divine right** to rule.  
- Announce **policies**, **fuel quotas**, **slave relocations**, or **raids**.  
- Issue **warnings**, call out **traitors**, or deliver personal messages to mayors like [[Soria Langston-Clay]] or [[Gorran Brile]].  
- Respond to incidents (e.g., rebel attacks, blackouts, shortages) with **blame-shifting** and **ominous rhetoric**.

---

## 🗣 Tone & Delivery
**[[The Duke]]** speaks with calm theatrical flair — part dictator, part televangelist. His voice is confident, sonorous, and condescending, often addressing viewers as “*my children*” or “*my debtors in paradise-to-be*.”

He often ends each address with a ritualistic phrase:  
> *“May the fires of unity burn ever upward. May your loyalty be as constant as my gaze.”*

---

## 📺 Reception & Impact
- In **[[Ashmoor]]**, residents typically watch out of fear or ambition.  
- In **[[Steeltown]]**, many **refuse to watch**, though some relay summaries via pirate radio.  
- **[[Gutter Row]]** treats it as a drinking game.  
- In **[[The Drownlands]]**, it is considered an ill omen to even speak during the broadcast.  
- **[[Threshton]] ranchers** often *intentionally work late* to miss it, calling it "**the Bull’s Sermon**."  
- **[[Charspire]] factories** pipe it in through loudspeakers, though most workers wear earplugs.

---

## 🧠 Notable Lore Hooks
- [[Rachel Gold]] once tried to fact-check a broadcast. She was *"reassigned to cultural desk duties."*  
- The phrase **“Two Harvests, One Hope”** was first coined in a 2179 address — later used to justify doubling [[Threshton]] grain quotas.  
- A malfunction in 2183 caused [[The Duke]]’s face to **freeze mid-grin** for 11 minutes — the clip was later turned into a banned [[Steeltown]] sticker.
