> *"They didn’t fight for territory. They fought for the last cup of clean water."*  
> – Recovered transmission, Year 2109

## 🌊 Background

The planet was already dying.  
The oceans were poisoned, the skies were cracked, and the aquifers had run dry.

In the final decades of civilization, most remaining freshwater sources were sold off under what was known as the **Global Hydration Accord** — a desperate treaty that handed control of water to corporate conglomerates in exchange for infrastructure repair.

The people revolted. The governments panicked.  
The corporations fortified.  
And war began.

---

## 🏴 Phase One: The Corporate Front

Private security forces armed with bleeding-edge tech enforced strict water zones. The world was carved into zones of control.

- **The North Reach**: First major flashpoint. Riots led to the collapse of three citystates.  
- **The Coil Rim**: Tech hubs where AI combat drones were first deployed.  
- **Flood Zones**: Heavily bombed river territories. Access denied to civilians.  
- **The Salt Wastes**: Deserts soaked in blood and broken desalination rigs.

---

## 🤖 Phase Two: The Machine Years

| Technology             | Role in the War |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Combat Drones**      | Controlled key rivers and dam zones  
| **Skimmer Raiders**    | Stole mobile reserves mid-transit  
| **Neural Infantry**    | Failed attempt at mind-linked soldiers  
| **Orbital Pulse Cannons** | Used to vaporize contested reservoirs

Most of this tech died with the cities that built them.

---

## 🧍 Phase Three: The Return to Blood

By 2118, the machines were gone — broken, turned rogue, or unpowered.  
And the world returned to the old ways.  
Boots. Guns. Trenches. Mass graves.

Water shipments were hijacked by the gallon. Whole battalions slaughtered to take a single well. And in the end, everyone was thirsty.

---

## 💥 Final Phase: The Tetra Ignition

When the **[[Tetra Bomb]]** was deployed across the **Midcontinent Line**, nothing survived.

Not the corporations.  
Not the governments.  
Not the war.

The world froze.  
And then it waited to be reborn.

## 🔗 Legacy

- No government has ever reformed.
- Fresh water remains the rarest commodity in [[DISGRACELAND]].
- Children are taught to fear thirst before fire.
- No one speaks of the Accord anymore — only the aftermath.
