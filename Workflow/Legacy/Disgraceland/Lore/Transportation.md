## Overview

Transportation in Disgraceland is a brutal mix of scarcity, improvisation, and control. With fuel tightly rationed and roads barely maintained, only the powerful ride in comfort — while the rest walk, limp, or saddle up.

---

## Fuel Economy

All known fuel reserves and refining operations are controlled by [[The Duke]], with refining centralized in [[Charspire]] under the iron grip of Mayor [[Gorren Brile]]

Fuel is distributed exclusively through state-run depots in each major territory, sold at severe markups. This keeps everyday citizens dependent, while also rewarding loyalty and punishing dissent.

Smuggling fuel is punishable by death — and still happens daily.

---

## Common Transportation Methods

- **Walking**: The most common way to get around. Simple. Free. Slow. Dangerous.
- **Pack Animals**: Horses, mules, and even oxen are making a slow return. Ranchers in [[Threshton]] have started quietly breeding stock again. Carts, carriages, and sleds are now a common sight in rural regions.
- **Scavenged Bikes & Carts**: Children, merchants, and drifters often use homemade wheelbarrow carts or two-wheeled junkbikes made from scrap.
- **Automobiles**: Rare. Frankenstein rigs built from old-world parts and scrap. Think Mad Max — armored panels, engine smoke, and heavy welds. Loud. Unpredictable. Respected.

## Road Conditions

Most highways are fractured, overgrown, or outright collapsed. Bridges are rare and fiercely protected. Those that remain are either toll-controlled or watched by [[Loyalist Guard]].

Travel outside safe zones is extremely risky — requiring guards, fuel, water, and luck.

---

## Black Market Travel

If you know the right people, you can:

- Rent a hidden fuel stash.
- Buy a seat on a roaming trader’s armored bus.
- Hire a stealth wagon convoy to smuggle you through enemy regions.

Just don’t ask questions. And always pay in advance.
