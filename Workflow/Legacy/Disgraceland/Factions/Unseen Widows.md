<h5>History of the Unseen Widows</h5>

> <h4>“Veerah will lead us all to the everlasting light.”</h4>

The Unseen Widows are a shadow that lingers over Disgraceland — silent, patient, and deadly. They are not a mere faction or band of raiders; they are a force born from sorrow and vengeance. Composed solely of women who have endured the worst of the world’s cruelty, the Widows move unseen through the ruins, striking without mercy and disappearing before the blood dries. Their name is both a warning and a promise. Many who cross them will not live to see their assassins.

The Widows trace their origin to Veerah, a self-proclaimed prophet who rose from the ashes of despair. After her vivid dreams of the future began to come true, she was declared a living oracle by those desperate to believe in something greater. But the Widows were not built on faith alone. Many of the women who now call her leader were once victims of the Duke’s brutality — slaves, prisoners, and survivors of unimaginable torment. When they had no one else, Veerah offered them purpose. In her name, they found strength.

Operating under a strict code of silence and loyalty, the Unseen Widows are masters of assassination, espionage, and psychological warfare. Veerah teaches them the art of moving without sound, of vanishing into shadows, and of striking down their enemies before they are even aware of the threat. Each Widow wears black, their faces partially veiled to mask their identities. Only Veerah remains unveiled, her golden hair and striking presence a symbol of the divine force they believe guides them. A gold bird emblem — the mark of the Widows — gleams on the chest of every member, a symbol of rebirth from the ashes of their past.

But it is not only steel that makes the Widows formidable. Veerah instructs her followers to use their femininity as a weapon. Beauty, charm, and seduction are as deadly as any blade in a world where desire blinds even the most powerful. The Widows infiltrate, manipulate, and dismantle their enemies from within, leaving ruin in their wake. While most of the women are trained assassins, others specialize in gathering intelligence, sowing fear, and undermining the Duke’s strongholds.

Their devotion to Veerah is absolute. Many see her as a living conduit of divine will, believing that her visions are proof of a higher power’s guidance. Dissent is rare, but those who question her prophecies are met with suspicion. Even the most trusted Widows, like Riley Thorne, Raina, and Risa, carry unspoken doubts about the true nature of Veerah’s visions — but to speak them aloud would risk exile or worse.

While their methods are brutal, the Widows justify their violence as necessary in their fight against the Duke’s tyranny. But the longer they operate from the shadows, the more the line between justice and vengeance blurs. For now, they remain a whispered legend, feared by the powerful and revered by the oppressed. In Disgraceland, mothers tell their children that the Widows only come for the wicked.

But in truth, the Widows come for anyone standing in their way.
