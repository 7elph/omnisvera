<h5>History of the Rustwater Raiders</h5>

> <h4>“Stormborn. Steel-fed. No graves on water.”</h4>

The [[Rustwater Raiders]] are Disgraceland’s most infamous aquatic marauders, their black sails a harbinger of violence and smoke. Born from betrayal and bound by blood, they sail where no one else dares, feeding on fear and salt.

Captain [[Lyla Bonetooth]] forged the Raiders from chaos. Her mutiny aboard a government patrol ship lit the first fire — when she seized the vessel and rechristened it [[The Black Marauder]], the era of the Raiders began. What followed was a campaign of calculated terror: raiding settlements, destroying convoys, and rewriting the rules of power in the bay.

Beside her sails the stone-faced [[Blacktide Cray]], a man said to have drowned once and clawed his way back. And behind the captain’s smile is [[Silas Vayne]], the crew’s voice in foreign waters and their dagger in the dark.

[[Zadie Adeyemi]] and [[Darius Adeyemi]], siblings with secrets, fuel the Raiders' dominance through invention, sabotage, and strategy. [[Salt-Eye Marlow]] — once a killer-for-hire, now a killer-by-oath — ensures discipline through violence.

Their reach spans far past the deck of the Marauder. They rule invisible harbors and hidden ports, unmarked on any map but whispered of in terror. No fortress is safe, no merchant unwatched. Even the [[The Duke]] treads lightly when the Raiders loom.

Their code is brutal: freedom at all costs, and loyalty unto death. Mutiny is met with blades. Weakness is thrown to the sea. But loyalty? Loyalty is treasure — and Captain Bonetooth rewards it with spoils and survival.

The Raiders live without chains. No kings. No borders. Just the wind, the blade, and the endless blue.

And still they sail — through mist and myth, leaving legends in their wake.
