## History of the Steeltown Wrecking Crew

><h4>“Pray to El Santo, the Wrecking Crew  is coming.”</h4>

The [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]] wasn’t born from ambition — it was born from grief.

Long before [[The Duke]] of Disgraceland claimed his iron throne, [[Steeltown]] was a place of industry and resilience. Steel mills once roared, their fires lighting the sky as men like [[Darrell Douglass]] worked tirelessly to provide for their families. The people of Steeltown didn’t have much, but they had each other — a bond forged in sweat and smoke.

But when the [[Tetra bomb]] fell and the water wars erupted, everything changed. The mills shut down, and the city crumbled. Fuel became scarce, and clean water even scarcer. The Duke, sensing opportunity, seized control of what little remained, imposing brutal taxes and using his enforcers to crush any resistance. When the people of Steeltown dared to stand up, the Duke made an example of them.

Among those victims was Darrell Douglass, a man who refused to bow. He led a strike against the Duke’s fuel rationing, calling for fair treatment. His courage inspired others, but the Duke had no tolerance for defiance. Darrell was beaten and left to die in the streets — a message to anyone who dared to challenge the regime.

But the Duke underestimated the spirit of Steeltown.

Darrell’s sons, Jimmy and [[Mark Jeffrey]], didn’t weep. They didn’t beg. They rose. Fueled by anger and the need for justice, Jimmy vowed to tear down the system that took their father. He gathered a handful of like-minded souls — fighters, mechanics, survivors — and together they formed the Steeltown Wrecking Crew. At first, they were nothing more than a band of rebels on patched-together bikes, striking back where they could and vanishing before the Duke’s forces could respond.

But word spread.

Stories of the Crew's defiance reached every corner of Disgraceland. People whispered of the brothers in black, of roaring engines and fires in the night. For the forgotten, the Wrecking Crew became a symbol of resistance. Jimmy, with his relentless fire, led the charge. Mark, ever the tactician, made sure they stayed one step ahead. They struck supply chains, liberated prisoners, and rerouted stolen resources to the people. And as their legend grew, so did their numbers.

The Steeltown Wrecking Crew welcomed outcasts and survivors — anyone willing to fight for something greater. Among the first to join was [[Abel May Jr.]], a towering force who had been left for dead by the Duke’s men. Jimmy and Mark saved his life, and from that day, Abel swore to stand by them. He became the Crew’s enforcer, a wall of muscle and loyalty.

Then came [[Joe Ameen]], the mechanical genius who kept the Crew’s bikes and rigs running. Joe hated the Duke’s control over the fuel supply, and his skills became invaluable in their war. The Crew rode faster and hit harder with Joe’s ingenuity fueling their machines.

[[Katie Kowalski]] was another pillar of the Crew. A self-taught doctor, she grew up alongside Mark and Joe. When the violence of the wasteland left its mark, it was Katie’s steady hands that patched the wounds. Her kindness tempered the Crew’s brutality, a reminder that not all battles are fought with steel.

Even as the Crew grew, their bonds remained unbreakable. Every patch earned was a promise — to fight, to protect, and to stand together. The Crew wasn’t just a rebellion. It was family.

But the Duke wasn’t blind. As the Wrecking Crew’s reputation spread, so did the Duke’s wrath. He sent his enforcers to burn their hideouts, slaughter their allies, and sow fear wherever the Crew’s name was spoken. Brady and Brody May, twisted remnants of the Duke’s experiments, were unleashed as his monstrous enforcers. The fact that they were Abel’s own cousins made the betrayal even more bitter.

Yet, no matter how much blood the Duke spilled, the Crew endured.

Jimmy’s calls for full rebellion grew louder. He believed the people of Disgraceland were ready to rise. But Mark, ever the cautious one, knew the price of war. Tension simmered between the brothers, though their love for each other kept it from boiling over.

In the face of hardship, the Crew’s spirit only strengthened. [[Selena Matissao]] joined the fight, a fierce warrior who had lost her husband to the Duke’s cruelty. Known as The Phoenix in the streets, she brought her tracking skills and ruthless efficiency to the Crew’s cause. When [[Lincoln Jones]] arrived, his quick thinking and fierce loyalty earned him a place among them — as well as the role of the Crew’s most celebrated cook.

Now, the Steeltown Wrecking Crew is more than a rebellion. They are a symbol of what Disgraceland could be — free from tyranny, united by the bonds of survival. They still ride the twisted highways, their engines roaring defiance against the Duke’s rule.

For Jimmy, the war is far from over. He dreams of the day the Duke falls, of a world where children no longer grow up in fear. Mark watches the horizon, knowing the hardest battles are yet to come. Abel, Joe, Katie, Selena, Lincoln, and every patched-in member stand ready — because to wear the Steeltown Wrecking Crew patch means to fight for something worth dying for.

And so, they ride.
