<h5>History of the Rancher Collective</h5>

> <h4>“Our mission is survival.”</h4>

Long before [[The Duke]] cemented his rule with rations and rifle barrels, [[Threshton]] was just dust, starvation, and wild cattle. After the [[Tetra Bomb]], most of the area fell silent — fields went fallow, families scattered, and the old supply chains turned to rust. What remained was fought over, stolen, or hoarded by those with the might to defend it.

But in the ruins of that scorched earth, after decades had healed what time could of the land, a man named [[Harp Langston]] planted a seed — literally and otherwise.

He built [[Threshton#Red Night Ranch|Red Night Ranch]] with scarred hands and the kind of fury only true survival breeds. While others traded bullets for bread, Harp raised cattle and coaxed wheat out of poisoned soil. His methods were brutal, his land cursed with sweat and bone, but somehow, it worked. He fed himself, then a few neighbors, then word started spreading: “There’s food in [[Threshton]].”

Soon, other ranches sprouted like weeds around Red Night — desperate men trying to mimic Harp’s stubborn miracle. A few succeeded. Most failed. But the ones who made it? Harp brought them to his table. Not out of charity. Out of strategy.

“We got two choices,” he’d said, leaning over a cracked map and a bottle of rotgut whiskey.  
“Fight each other ‘til we all starve… or link arms and deal from strength.”

Thus, the [[Rancher Collective]] was born — five ranches banding together, agreeing to share resources, protect their convoys, and negotiate food distribution as one voice. And it worked. [[The Duke]], still consolidating power in [[Ashmoor]], needed a reliable food source — and the Collective had it.

So a deal was struck:  
30% for the ranchers. 70% for the Duke.  
Harsh, but better than bullets.

Over time, ranches rose and fell, and with them, membership shifted. Only Harp remained constant — the scarred sentinel of Red Night Ranch. Others came and went. Turner Anderson, now the largest landowner in Threshton, brought size and steady leadership. Freddy Blanchard brought heart and muscle. Beau DuNoir brought silver-tongued deals and backroom charm. And then, like a bad smell in a locked room, came Danny Dandelion, who conned his way into Snake Kiss Ranch and a seat at the table.

The Collective operates under simple but ironclad rules:

- Majority vote rules. Always.  
      
    
- No one acts alone in major trade matters.  
      
    
- All members must contribute to the food output.  
      
    
- And every meeting ends with a shot of whiskey — no matter how heated the vote.  
      
    

They are not friends. They are not brothers.  
They are a working alliance, forged by fire and held together by the brittle string of necessity.  
Without them, Disgraceland starves.  
With them, it teeters on the edge of rebellion.

And everyone knows — the day the [[Rancher Collective]] falls apart…  
the whole damn region goes with it.
