<h5>History of the Loyalist Guard</h5>

> <h4>“Obedience is order. Order is survival.”</h4>

In the scorched days following the [[Tetra Bomb]],  [[The Duke]] knew he needed more than fear to hold what he’d seized. He needed *control*. He found it in a man named [[General Colt]], a former military tactician with a broken moral compass and a talent for discipline.

Together, they created the Loyalist Guard — not a militia, but a machine.

The Guard began as a personal protection unit for the Duke, drawn from the dregs of old-world armies, ex-mercenaries, and psychopaths. Colt molded them into an elite force: brutal, obedient, and unquestioning. Every recruit underwent reconditioning. Every mission ended in silence. Over time, the Guard grew into a nation-spanning power — a symbol of fear etched into the fabric of every region in Disgraceland.

To enforce the Duke’s rule more effectively, specialized subgroups were deployed into each territory:

#### **Blueforce** 
– Stationed in [[Steeltown]]; used for riot control and Wrecking Crew suppression.  
#### **Greenforce** 
– Based in [[Ashmoor]]; guards the elite, runs checkpoints, and oversees Silo operations.  
#### **Blackforce** 
– Active peacekeepers in [[GUTTER ROW]]; led by Captain [[Kullen Dane]] works with informants, black markets, and slave transports.  
#### **Redforce** 
– Brutal enforcers in [[Charspire]]; protects Mayor Brile and ensures factory compliance.  
#### **Amberforce** 
– Operating in [[Threshton]]; watches ranchers and tracks illegal food supplies.

At the Guard’s head remains General Colt, a man feared even by the Duke’s inner circle. His enforcer, **[[Sgt. Rottwyler]]**, handles discipline internally with savage precision. Beneath them operate a growing roster of loyal agents: [[Raynar Ventura]], silent but surgical; [[Jacoban Clay]], a political insider and family to the feared Clays of [[GUTTER ROW]]; and [[Emily Burton]], the cold, calculating logistics coordinator who ensures the Duke’s will moves like clockwork.

More terrifying still are the Duke’s “fail-safes” — experimental soldiers twisted by science and desperation. [[Brady]] and [[Brody May]], former men turned monsters, unleashed only when terror alone won’t do.

For many, the Loyalist Guard *is* the law. For others, it’s the boot that never lifts. They are feared, hated, and unrelenting.

But above all else — they are loyal.
