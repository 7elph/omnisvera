## Biography

><h4>“You can’t kill what moves in two places at once.”</h4>

Raina and Risa were born into the unforgiving wastes of Disgraceland, twin daughters of a wandering trader and a mother who died before their third birthday. From their earliest days, the sisters were inseparable, bound by the unspoken understanding that survival meant staying together. Their father led a nomadic life, navigating the scorched remnants of the old world, bartering stolen scraps and scavenged goods. It wasn’t a life of comfort, but it was a life.

That life ended the day their caravan crossed paths with the Duke’s enforcers. Accused of trading contraband and withholding supplies, their father was executed without trial. The twins were dragged away, separated, and sold into servitude. For years, they endured lives of torment — Raina forced into hard labor, Risa into the twisted entertainment of the Duke’s wealthy allies. But even apart, the sisters’ bond never broke. They clung to the hope of finding one another, whispering silent promises beneath the stars.

When the time came, they made good on those promises. Raina escaped first, slaughtering a guard with a jagged scrap of metal. Risa’s liberation followed soon after, her captors falling to the ruthless precision of a stolen dagger. Reunited and stained with the blood of their tormentors, the sisters fled into the night. They were no longer victims — they were vengeance made flesh.

It was Veerah who found them next, drawn by the shadows they left in their wake. The Unseen Widows offered them sanctuary and purpose. Raina, fierce and unrelenting, became a master of close-quarters combat, wielding her blades with ruthless efficiency. Risa, calculating and precise, embraced the art of silent death, her throwing stars finding their mark without fail. Together, they are a force of nature — twin specters feared even among the Duke’s most hardened enforcers.

Though bound by their loyalty to the Widows, the sisters remain wary of Veerah’s unwavering belief in her visions. Unlike some of their sisters, Raina and Risa do not follow blindly. They follow each other. The world has taken everything from them, but as long as they stand side by side, the sisters of the Unseen Widows will never be broken.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Raina is raw fire — quick to strike, blunt, and driven by instinct. Risa is the cooling shadow — calm, clever, and dangerously observant. Together, they form a perfect balance of chaos and precision. They speak in unison when they wish to intimidate, but often communicate without words at all.
**Vices:**  
They don't sleep unless the other is watching. Both suffer from fragmented nightmares, often reliving their separate captivities. Raina breaks things when angry. Risa journals in code no one else understands.
**Abilities:**  
Raina is a brutal melee fighter — excels in confined combat, knife work, and disarming enemies with speed and strength. Risa specializes in ranged precision, poisons, and stealth infiltration. Together, they perform tandem attacks that overwhelm opponents before they understand what’s happening.
**Secrets:**  
Each keeps scars the other has never asked about. Risa knows one of her captors still lives, and she hasn’t told Raina. Raina sometimes wonders if they'd survive being separated again — and dreads the answer.
**Beliefs:**  
They believe in each other before anything else. Their faith in Mirella is quiet, ritualistic, and mostly tied to Veerah's influence — but they follow the Widow creed as long as it aligns with their bond.

## Role In Disgraceland
Raina and Risa are among the most feared Widows in the field. Their synchronized tactics make them nearly impossible to counter — one distracts, the other kills. They are often deployed for missions requiring shock value, disruption, and psychological warfare. Their presence alone is enough to cause panic among Loyalist ranks. Though they outwardly serve Veerah, their true allegiance has always been to each other. To lose one would be to lose both — and if that ever happens, Disgraceland may tremble.
