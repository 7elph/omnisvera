## Biography

><h4>“The stars don’t scream. They whisper. And if you’re foolish enough to ignore them… they burn.”</h4>

Veerah was once no different from the countless forgotten souls of Disgraceland — another survivor scraping by in a world that had long since abandoned hope. But everything changed the night the visions began. Dreams of fragmented moments, distant faces, and twisted landscapes plagued her sleep. At first, she thought them the fevered remnants of hunger and exhaustion. But when those dreams started to unfold in reality — each detail as precise as she had seen — Veerah knew she had been touched by something far beyond comprehension.

She called it a gift, though whether it was a blessing or a curse remains uncertain. In a world shattered by war and ruin, certainty is a rare commodity. Yet Veerah’s followers see her as a prophet, a vessel for divine truth. To them, her dreams are the whispered will of a higher power. The Unseen Widows formed around her, not merely as warriors but as devoted disciples. They believe every word that passes Veerah’s lips, seeing her as a guiding light in the perpetual darkness of Disgraceland.

But Veerah is no mere oracle. Beneath the gilded tiara that crowns her golden hair is a mind sharpened by hardship. Before the Widows, she was a ghost, moving through the shadows, mastering the art of silent death. She learned the value of patience — the perfect kill is never rushed. Every flicker of light, every shifting shadow, became her allies. Now, she passes these lessons to her sisters, transforming them into assassins that strike without warning. They wear their veils and black garb like armor, their beauty a weapon as sharp as any blade. Veerah teaches them to use the desires of others to their advantage, understanding that seduction is as deadly as steel.

Despite her growing legend, there are whispers of doubt that even Veerah cannot silence. Each vision takes its toll, leaving her exhausted and frayed. She claims to hear the voice of a higher being, but in the stillness of the night, she sometimes wonders if the voice is merely her own. The Widows know nothing of her uncertainty — to them, she is infallible. Yet the burden of prophecy weighs heavy. Every life taken by her word, every drop of blood spilled in her name, carves a deeper wound within her soul.

Still, Veerah remains resolute. The Duke’s tyranny cannot go unanswered, and Disgraceland’s suffering will not endure forever. Whether her path is righteous or not, she will follow her visions until the end. And when the time comes, the Widows will be ready — their hands steady, their blades sharp, and their faith unwavering. For to defy Veerah is to defy fate itself.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Veerah is poised and enigmatic, radiating an eerie calm even in chaos. She’s introspective, deliberate, and unshakably confident in public — yet harbors private doubts she shares with no one. Her voice is soft, her words carry weight, and her silence says even more.

**Vices:**  
Keeps her pain to herself, refusing rest or comfort. Addicted to control — not power, but control. Occasionally indulges in rare, potent opiates to silence the visions
**Abilities:**  
Vivid, often traumatic visions of the future; always accurate. Trained in silent movement, blade work, and psychological warfare. Inspires unwavering loyalty and obedience in her followers
**Secrets:**  
She is no longer sure if the voice in her dreams is divine… or her own madness
**Beliefs:**  
Her visions are sacred truths, whether from a god, a force, or something else

## Role In Disgraceland
Veerah is the leader and prophet of the **Unseen Widows**, a cult-like faction of all-female assassins operating from the Drownlands. Feared, worshipped, and untouchable, she influences events from the shadows, shaping Disgraceland’s future one death at a time. Whether messiah or madwoman, she stands as a symbol of vengeance, destiny, and quiet terror.
