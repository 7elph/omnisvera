## Biography

><h4>"Some people were born to fight. I was born to disappear."</h4>

Riley Thorne’s life was marked by loss before she could fully understand it. Born to a scavenger mother in the decaying wastelands of Disgraceland, she spent her earliest years rummaging through the bones of the old world. Her mother, fierce and resourceful, did her best to shield Riley from the brutal reality of the Duke’s rule. But no one could stay hidden forever. When Riley was just ten, her mother was captured and publicly executed by the Duke’s enforcers for stealing from one of the regime’s supply convoys. The image of her mother’s lifeless body swinging from the gallows burned into Riley’s memory, igniting a hatred that never cooled.

Alone and afraid, Riley learned to survive by vanishing into the shadows. The ruins of the past became her refuge — broken concrete towers and rusted steel skeletons offering concealment. She scavenged scraps of food and salvaged discarded tools, slowly honing her skills. Over the years, whispers of rebellion reached her ears. Small pockets of resistance, desperate to overthrow the Duke, began to stir. By her late teens, Riley had aligned herself with one such group, using her stealth and cunning to sabotage supply lines and strike fear into the regime. But her actions earned her powerful enemies. During a high-stakes assassination attempt on one of the Duke’s top commanders, Riley was captured and left for dead, her body battered and broken.

Yet death did not claim her. It was Veerah, enigmatic leader of the Unseen Widows, who found Riley on the brink of her end. Under Veerah’s care, Riley recovered. The Widows offered her something she hadn't known since her mother’s death — a family. With them, she became a weapon of silent vengeance. Her skill with daggers and short blades grew unmatched, and her ability to move unseen became legendary. Though she serves the Widows with loyalty, a lingering doubt remains. While Veerah’s visions guide the faction’s every step, Riley cannot shake the unease that comes with blind faith. She remains stoic and guarded, her heart still burdened by the weight of the past.

Despite the blood she has spilled, there is still a spark of compassion within her. Riley fiercely protects her sisters in the Widows, seeing reflections of herself in the broken women Veerah has gathered. But as tensions rise in Disgraceland and the Duke’s hold tightens, Riley finds herself at a crossroads. Vengeance once defined her, but now she questions whether there is more to fight for. The path forward is uncertain, but one truth remains — Riley Thorne is not done surviving.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Riley is cold on the outside, simmering beneath with quiet intensity. She speaks little, listens much, and watches always. Distrustful by nature, she trusts only those who've bled beside her. Her loyalty, once earned, is absolute — but rarely given.
**Vices:**  
Sleeplessness. Nightmares keep her awake, and when they don’t, she refuses rest out of guilt. She sharpens her blades obsessively, a ritual of control. Distrusts comfort.
**Abilities:**  
Elite stealth operative. Master of knives and short blades. Can vanish in plain sight. Deep intuition for traps, movement, and enemy intent. Skilled in sabotage and infiltration.
**Secrets:**  
Doesn’t fully believe in Veerah’s visions — and sometimes thinks about leaving. Still keeps a piece of her mother’s scarf hidden in her gear.
**Beliefs:**  
Faith in Mirella, but more as metaphor than god. Believes in survival. In sisterhood. In making the powerful bleed.

## Role In Disgraceland
Riley Thorne serves as one of the Widows' deadliest field agents — their silent blade, sent when the mission calls for precision, patience, and vanishing without a trace. She does not preach. She does not lead. She kills. And yet her actions hold weight among the Widows, especially the younger ones who look to her as proof that survival and strength are possible after tragedy. Where Veerah brings faith, Riley brings grit. Where Veerah inspires awe, Riley inspires caution. She is the shadow to the Prophet’s light — unseen, but always there.
