## Biography

><h4>"Cattle die. Crops wither. But a good lie? A good lie’ll outlive the both of us."</h4>

A man with a too-slick grin and a voice like honey poured over a cactus, **Danny Dandelion** drifted into [[Threshton]] under one name and left with another. No one knows where he came from. Some say he once rode with the [[Rustwater Raiders]], others claim he used to work Duke’s checkpoints before skipping out with a pouch of stolen ration chits. Either way, he ain’t from around here — and everyone wishes he’d leave the same way he came: quietly and fast.

The [[THRESHTON#Snake Kiss Ranch|Snake Kiss Ranch]] was once a peaceful patch of land run by a kindly widow named Miss Greta Harp. Danny showed up selling some miracle tonic to help her ailing lungs, and within six months, she’d “voluntarily” handed over the deed. A week later, she was found dead from a “fall.” No one could prove a damn thing. But everybody knows.

He wears a fake name like he wears his smile — big, loud, and distracting. He talks a mile a minute, never stops moving, and somehow always ends up with more cards in his hand than he started with. The townsfolk don’t trust him, and the [[Rancher Collective]] sure as hell doesn’t respect him, but [[Beau DuNoir]] keeps him around for the entertainment — and maybe, just maybe, because Danny knows a thing or two Beau doesn’t want whispered.

Danny has no cattle to speak of, and the land he owns ain’t worth half what the deed says. But he’s got charm, hustle, and an uncanny ability to walk away from things other men would get buried over.  
[[Turner Anderson]] would shoot him on sight if it wouldn’t start something ugly.  [[Freddy Blanchard]] won’t shake his hand without checking his fingers afterward.  

But somehow… Danny’s still standing.

## Personality

Danny is a born talker — fast, oily, and always one step ahead of someone else’s suspicion. He dresses like a merchant prince on market day, but he smells like dust and old rum. He thrives in chaos and disappears when things get too real. His habits include cheating at cards, sipping stolen liquor, flirting with danger, and worming into conversations where he doesn’t belong. He lies like other men breathe, and he enjoys every second of it.
He can't sit still, always pacing, always plotting. He’s the type to toast you with one hand while robbing you with the other. No one knows if he sleeps — and if he does, it’s probably with a knife under the pillow and a back door left open.

## Role In Disgraceland
Danny Dandelion is the rot on the roots of the Rancher Collective — tolerated only because removing him might cause more trouble than he’s worth. He brings nothing to the table but secrets, survival instincts, and just enough blackmail to keep himself out of a shallow grave. He is the wildcard no one trusts, yet no one can quite afford to kill. His true value might lie in what he knows — and who he’s willing to betray when the time comes.
