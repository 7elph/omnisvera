## Biography

><h4>“A man’s worth ain’t counted in coin or cattle. It’s in how many mouths he’s fed and how many devils he’s held off ‘til the sun comes up.”</h4>

Turner Anderson was born under a copper sky in the scorched outskirts of [[Threshton]], back when the region was still choking on dust and lawlessness in the immediate fallout of the [[Tetra Bomb]]. His father was one of the first to settle after the skies cleared, carving a patch of land out of the dead soil and coaxing life from it with blood and fire. Turner grew up with blisters on his hands and a rifle slung across his back — by age thirteen, he’d already buried two rustlers and a mother lost to the cough.

The [[THRESHTON#Double Cross Ranch|Double Cross Ranch]] became legend during the first wave of post-bomb settlements. Named after the cattle brand his father carved from scrap iron — two crude Xs overlapping — the ranch stood for resilience and survival. Turner led efforts to irrigate the fields using a jerry-rigged network of salvaged piping and old-world farm tech. He was the first to successfully grow wheat in Tetra-scorched soil and the first to trade cattle beyond Threshton’s borders. But it was never just about survival for Turner — it was about pride. Dignity. Feeding people who’d never known anything but hunger.

As the [[Rancher Collective]] formed to organize the increasingly fragile supply lines, Turner naturally stepped into a leadership role. The other four ranchers — each tough in their own right — respected his steady hand, his iron-bound code, and his willingness to ride into danger to protect a shipment of grain or a rusted-out water pump. Some say the first meeting of the Collective was settled over whiskey and blood in Turner’s barn, after he challenged a land baron to a knife duel to stop a water hoarding scheme. Turner won — didn’t kill the man, but left him without his trigger finger.

He’s seen the Duke’s claws tighten around Threshton year after year — tariffs, fuel rations, guards posted at silo checkpoints. He understands the deal: the Duke supplies power, fuel, and some semblance of order in exchange for the region’s food. But Turner knows damn well that it’s extortion dressed up as stability. Still, breaking from [[The Duke]] would mean starvation for half the map. That conflict lives in his eyes — every time he signs off on a shipment destined for [[Ashmoor]].

He wears black like mourning cloth. Lost his only son in a skirmish near [[GUTTER ROW]] — a fight over food the boy never should’ve been in. That grief made Turner harder, quieter. Now he rides alone most days, inspecting the herds, keeping the young ranch hands in line, making sure no one skims from the weight scales.

Some say if there ever is a rebellion in Threshton, it won’t start with the loud mouths or the gun hawks. It’ll start when Turner Anderson stops sending crates of grain to Ashmoor.

And when that happens, the whole damn food chain might come crashing down.

## Personality

> [!infobox]
>
> ![[duke3.png|260]]

**Temperament:**  
Stoic and principled. Turner speaks little, listens deeply, and weighs every decision like it could break a man. He is the calm in the storm — until pushed too far.

**Vices:**  
Whiskey before sunup. Long, lonely rides. The need to fix everything himself.

**Abilities:**  
Master survivalist and rancher; pioneered post-Tetra agriculture in Threshton. Skilled marksman and knife fighter; once ended a land dispute with a blade, not a bullet. - Natural leader; commands respect without ever asking for it.

**Secrets:**  
Has met privately with **Freddy Blanchard** more than once to discuss what happens _after_ the Duke falls.

**Beliefs:**  
Justice is non-negotiable. The Duke is a disease, and Disgraceland won’t heal until he’s ripped out root and stem. Jimmy believes in the people — and that they’re worth every scar.

## Role In Disgraceland
Turner Anderson is the heart and backbone of Threshton. A living symbol of what the land used to be — and what it still could become. He holds the food supply chain together by sheer will and grit, walking the razor’s edge between obedience to the Duke and quiet rebellion. If Turner ever tips the scales, Disgraceland won’t be able to ignore it.
