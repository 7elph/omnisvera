## Biography

><h4>“They all scream the same in the end. Some just take longer to get there.”</h4>


Salt-Eye Marlow is what happens when survival forgets to bring a conscience. Bald, hulking, and half-blind, he walks the Black Marauder like a ghost who never learned how to die. His milky eye gave him the name — the stories gave him the fear.

He once served another crew. That crew no longer exists. After a failed stand against Lyla Bonetooth’s Raiders, Marlow knelt in the blood of his comrades and offered to switch sides. Lyla said yes… but only after he slit their throats himself. He did it with a smile.

Now, Salt-Eye is the Raiders’ mad dog — the one they keep leashed until a message needs sending or a body needs breaking. He laughs through executions, jokes mid-interrogation, and collects trophies from those he’s put down — teeth, fingers, tags. Nobody knows what the trophies mean. Maybe not even him.

He’s loyal to Lyla, but not because of belief. Because she lets him _be_. And in a world that chews up outcasts like him, that’s the closest thing he’s got to religion.

Even Silas Vayne keeps a wary eye on him. Because while Salt-Eye might be a dog… nobody knows who he’ll bite next.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Unstable, gleefully violent, and always grinning. Marlow is the kind of man who whistles while flaying someone.
**Vices:**  
Betrayal, sadism, chaos. He lives for the breaking point — yours, not his.
**Abilities:**  
Master of close-quarters brutality and bloodletting. Pain doesn’t deter him — it feeds him. He tracks wounded targets by scent and finishes them slow.
**Secrets:**  
Keeps trophies from kills and whispers to them. Rumors say he’s making a list — or maybe it’s a prayer. Or a tally. No one wants to ask.
**Beliefs:**  
Life has no rules. Codes are chains. Survival is the only gospel — and Lyla Bonetooth is the first person to ever give him freedom instead of a cage.

## Role In Disgraceland
**Salt-Eye Marlow** is the Raiders’ nightmare unleashed — the one you send to prove a point. He’s not trusted. He’s not liked. But when you need to paint a deck red, he’s the brush.
