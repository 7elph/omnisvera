## Biography

><h4>“I’m not trying to win. I’m just trying to make sure someone tells the truth after the fire.”  </h4>

Marlon Lambert grew up in **Threshton**, raised by a father who printed grain receipts and a mother who ran messages for the Rancher Collective. He learned to read on old ration slips and grew up knowing that what people _write down_ matters more than what they say.

He moved to [[CHARSPIRE]] to work for the _Tribucia Tribute_, where he quickly made a name for himself digging into agricultural corruption and Loyalist zoning scandals. Then **[[Rachel Gold]]** showed up — and the bar moved. She wrote fire. He wrote fact. She was headline ink. He was page three.

Instead of resenting her, he fell for her. Not instantly, not with flowers — but with long nights watching her retype whole paragraphs, yelling at the press chief, storming out of the archive room muttering about “cover-ups and cowards.” He’s still in love. But he stays quiet.

He’s also become a regular at **The Hollow House**, where **Shana** feeds him breadcrumbs and occasionally lets him sleep in the back room when someone’s chasing him for asking the wrong questions.

Marlon isn’t trying to be the best.  
He’s just trying to make sure **someone remembers the truth** when Rachel’s gone.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Reserved, thoughtful, slightly awkward. He doesn’t interrupt. He listens. Always scribbling in his notepad like he’s transcribing your soul. The kind of guy who knows exactly what’s going on — and pretends he doesn’t until it’s too late for you.

**Vices:**  
Cigarettes. Whiskey. [[Rachel Gold]]. He visits the **[[Hollow House]]** weekly — sometimes for a drink, sometimes for a tip, sometimes just to see **[[Shana]]** smirk when he asks the wrong questions.

**Abilities:**  
Investigative journalist with deep connections in [[Threshton]], [[Gutter Row]], and [[Ashmoor]]. Can blend into a room and make people forget he’s there — until his article drops. Keeps a coded shorthand that only he understands.

**Secrets:**  
Has written drafts of love letters to Rachel that he never sends. Keeps a personal journal separate from his reporting — filled with observations about Rachel, Shana, and the growing unrest. Fears the day Rachel disappears chasing a story, because he won’t survive it

**Beliefs:**  
The story matters more than the byline. Rachel deserves to be protected — even if she’d never ask for it

## Role In Disraceland
Marlon Lambert is **Disgraceland’s second-best journalist** — and he knows it. Working in the same newsroom as **[[Rachel Gold]]**, he’s talented, respected, and entirely overlooked… which makes him **very dangerous** in his own way. He’s a quiet operator. He asks the right questions, earns the right trust, and knows how to disappear until the headline drops. He’s also hopelessly in love with Rachel — and has been for years. She doesn’t know. And if she does, she’s too focused on the story to care. So Marlon does what he always does: lights a smoke, follows the lead, and keeps the flame buried under copy edits.
