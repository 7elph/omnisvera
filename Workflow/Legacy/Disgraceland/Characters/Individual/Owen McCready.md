## Biography

><h4>“I don’t care who’s running the show, long as I get paid and nobody screws with my tower. You want the truth? Don’t look for it on TV.”  </h4>

Owen “Switch” McCready is the reluctant lifeblood of [[TTV Studios]], the last broadcast engineer in a world that barely remembers television. Fifty-eight but looks older, he’s wiry, stoop-shouldered, with permanent bags under his eyes and nicotine-stained fingers. His voice is hoarse, his wit sharper than the broken glass around the [[CHARSPIRE#Gravenwild|Gravenwild]] streets. He was running illegal radio, hiding from every government and corporation until [[The Duke]]’s people found him and gave him an offer he couldn’t refuse: fix the tower, run the shows, and keep his head down.

Switch is seen as a necessary evil by the regime and an enigma to the citizens — a guy who seems to exist in the walls, always lurking behind cables or vanishing into the static. He’s never at ease except when buried in tangled wires or soldering something by flashlight. Most folks have no idea if he’s loyal, broken, or just too tired to care.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Blunt, antisocial, and unflappable under pressure. Switch is not mean, but he’s got no patience for small talk or bullshit. Prefers silence to company, except for a late-night drink with one of the crew who actually knows which end of a soldering iron gets hot. If you waste his time, he’ll let you know. If you show up to help, he’ll grumble but let you stay.

**Vices:**  
Bourbon, hand-rolled cigarettes, and scavenged painkillers. Sleeps in the equipment room more nights than not, bottle in hand. His biggest vice, though, is isolation: Switch avoids people, feelings, and especially hope, burying himself in work and routine.

**Abilities:**  
A natural with anything electrical or mechanical, from broadcast towers to shortwave radios and analog security cams. Can fix almost anything with spare wire, scrap metal, and tape. Has an uncanny knack for reading a situation — he can smell trouble before it happens, and knows how to keep a low profile when the wrong people are sniffing around.

**Secrets:**  
Keeps a private shortwave set tuned to “forbidden” frequencies — sometimes intercepts rebel transmissions or pirate music, never says a word. He secretly recorded The Duke’s first address at TTV, stashing the original tape in a hidden box “for insurance.” Once helped a resistance runner sneak out by faking a signal blackout. Most dangerous secret: he’s got enough dirt on the Loyalist Guards to ruin several careers, but no desire to be a hero.

**Beliefs:**  
Trusts machines more than people, and believes survival means staying invisible. Cynical about politics; sees The Duke as “just another bastard in charge.” Deep down, though, Switch believes in freedom of information — but he’s convinced trying to fight the system outright just gets you killed.

## Role In Disgraceland
Switch is the only reason the signal reaches every home, bar, and bunker in Tribucia. He keeps TTV running, not out of loyalty, but because nobody else can. Both The Duke’s men and the general public leave him alone — he’s too useful to threaten, too strange to befriend.
