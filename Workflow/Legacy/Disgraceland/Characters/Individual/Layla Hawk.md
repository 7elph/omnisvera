## Biography

><h4>"Sometimes the truth doesn’t scream. Sometimes it just slips in the back door and sits real quiet until someone notices."  </h4>

[[Layla Hawk]] wasn’t supposed to survive [[Gutter Row]]. Orphaned young after a mugging turned slaughter left her alone in a world that eats the weak, she grew up learning to mask pain with poise and bury rage beneath reason. She was book-smart, quick with her tongue, and relentless in pursuit of the truth. After clawing her way into the [[Tribucia Tribute]] as a junior reporter, she built a reputation for fearless journalism, earning cautious admiration from editors and quiet fear from corrupt bureaucrats. Her stories cut deep — until the wrong one made the right person nervous.

That’s when [[Owen McCready]] came calling.

Plucked from the Tribute by McCready and installed as the co-anchor of [[TTV Evening News]] beside the ever-smooth [[Warren Scottyard]], Layla was polished up and paraded out — the perfect symbol of progress, of diversity, of opportunity. What The Duke never realized was that Layla wasn’t just playing along. Every night, as she smiles through stories crafted to maintain the Loyalist illusion, she slips in tiny seeds of resistance — careful word choices, pointed phrasing, silent glances. Enough to stir suspicion in the right minds. Enough to remind the oppressed that someone up there sees them.

Warren knows what she’s doing. It gnaws at him, but he hasn’t ratted her out — yet. Whether it’s respect, fear, or something deeper, neither of them talks about it.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Calm, clever, and sharp as a scalpel. Layla speaks softly but never aimlessly. She knows how to mask fury with a smirk and is more dangerous with a sentence than most are with a blade.

**Vices:**  
She drinks alone after broadcasts, writes poetry she never shares, and sometimes sabotages her own safety just to feel in control again.

**Abilities:**  
Razor-sharp intuition for subtext and danger. Master communicator — able to say ten things without saying one. Deep investigative instincts. She still has old Tribute sources who feed her real news.

**Secrets:**  
Keeps a hidden journal cataloging Loyalist crimes and corruption.

**Beliefs:**  
Freedom must be fought for, even in silence. Words are weapons, and truth is only dangerous when people are ready to hear it.

## Role In Disgraceland
The voice behind the velvet curtain, Layla is the quiet rebellion inside TTV — a survivor turned signal flare, broadcasting hope with every breath.
