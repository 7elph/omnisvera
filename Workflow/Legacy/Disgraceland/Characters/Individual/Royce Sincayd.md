## Biography

><h4>“Sometimes survival means knowing when to hit 'delete' instead of 'print.'”  </h4>

Once a firebrand of post-war journalism, **Royce Sincayd** founded the *[[Tribucia Tribute]]* with nothing but a broken printing press, a pocket full of ink cartridges, and a vision: truth above all. In the early years after the Long Winter, he was revered as a voice of resistance and integrity — exposing corruption, giving voice to the starving, and holding fledgling regional powers accountable.

But time wears on even the sharpest ideals. Decades of veiled threats, missing colleagues, and slow, suffocating pressure from [[The Duke]] chipped away at Royce’s resolve. These days, he still calls the shots in the newsroom, but everyone knows the *Tribute* won't publish anything directly negative about The Duke. He tells himself it’s survival. That it’s better to bend than break.

He walks the halls of his paper with an old man’s limp and a heavy conscience, watching as younger, hungrier reporters like [[Rachel Gold]] test the boundaries he no longer has the strength to push. The presses still roll. But what they print isn’t always the truth anymore — just the truth he’s allowed to tell.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Measured, weary, calculating. Royce used to speak in fire; now he speaks in sighs.  

**Vices:**  
Cheap cigars, self-pity, and willful ignorance when it suits him.  

**Abilities:**  
Master editor and political survivor. Knows how to shape a story — or bury it.  

**Secrets:**  
Keeps a locked drawer of unpublished exposés, including a damning file on [[Jacoban Clay]] and internal TTV memos. He may have been directly warned by [[The Duke]] years ago — and caved.  

**Beliefs:**  
Still believes in truth, deep down. But believes more in staying alive to print another day.  


## Role In Disgraceland
A once-proud truth-teller turned compromised gatekeeper of the press. His silence props up The Duke as much as his old words once challenged him.
