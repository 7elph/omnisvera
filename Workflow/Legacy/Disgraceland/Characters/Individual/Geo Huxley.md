## Biography

><h4>"If you’re looking for justice, call a preacher.  If you want answers, close the door and pour a drink." </h4>

[[Geo Huxley]] was born into the ash and clanging chaos of [[Steeltown]], the son of a boilermaker and a mother who vanished when he was nine. He grew up among soot-choked alleys and broken floodlights, learning how to read a room faster than he could read a book. When the mill whistle blew, the screams usually followed. He learned not to flinch.

By sixteen, he was drifting through [[Gutter Row]], running petty errands for dealers, junkers, and worse. Never joined a gang. Never pledged to the [[Loyalist Guard]]. Never looked for trouble — but it kept finding him. He was the kind of kid who noticed things others didn’t. The kind of man who didn’t ask questions — until he started.

He found his calling the day a half-dead girl collapsed in his doorway. She’d escaped from a transport van near [[Three Nail Market]], beaten and branded. No one was looking for her. No one cared. He got her to safety, tracked the route she came from, and burned the warehouse down with the men still inside.

After that, people started coming to him.

Missing daughters. Murdered drifters. Loyalist cover-ups. [[Geo Huxley]] never asked for coin upfront — if you were desperate enough to find him, he figured you were already broke. He built his office above a pawnshop in Vileby, and filled the walls with case notes, tattered maps, and the sound of rain hitting busted glass.

His reputation grew in the shadows. The man who didn't smile. Who always asked the right question. Who sometimes left with a knife and came back bloody but silent.

He doesn’t carry a badge. Doesn’t need to. And he only straps on a sidearm when he knows he’s walking into hell. But he’s walked into hell before — and these days, he walks in without blinking.

There’s talk that he’s building something. A private ledger of every quiet atrocity swept under the rug in [[Disgraceland]]. A case too big for any one man. But he keeps at it, one truth at a time, one body at a time, one final answer at the end of the trail.

Because [[Geo Huxley]] doesn’t care if justice is dead. He’s chasing ghosts anyway.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Quiet but razor-edged. [[Geo Huxley]] is always two steps ahead in a conversation, watching for the lie behind the eyes. Speaks with deliberate pauses, as if measuring every word for weight and consequence. Doesn’t bluff. Doesn’t rattle. Uses sarcasm like a blade, but never wastes it. Trusts no one, but listens to everyone.

**Vices:**  
Chain-smokes hand-rolled bitterroot cigs. Keeps a bottle of contraband charcoal whiskey under his desk, "for insomnia." Collects unsolved case files and reads them like scripture. Has a self-destructive habit of poking into Loyalist business just to see who twitches.

**Abilities:**  
Exceptional investigator — sharp memory, pattern recognition, and bullshit detection. Knows the city’s underground better than most crime lords. Proficient in knife fighting, ambush tactics, and going completely unnoticed. Keeps an old voice recorder on him to document every case and confession.

**Secrets:**  
Still haunted by a girl he couldn’t save in [[Threshton]] — her name shows up in the margins of every notebook. Once worked briefly for the Loyalists as a contract snitch, before turning on them after uncovering what they did to his handler. There’s a bounty on his head in [[Ashmoor]], quietly authorized by someone high up.

**Beliefs:**  
Justice is dead, but truth still limps around out there — and it matters. The Duke’s world is built on fear, and fear makes people stupid. People don’t need saving, they need someone who’ll walk through fire to find the goddamn matches.

## Role Details
**[[Geo Huxley]]** is the unofficial bloodhound of [[Disgraceland]] — a relic of a better conscience. He’s not a revolutionary, not a hero, just a man obsessed with the truth and the cracks where it hides. When a mother loses her son, when a worker vanishes in [[Charspire]], when a Loyalist says it was a suicide but you know better — you go to Geo. He’ll listen. He’ll find them. And if someone’s responsible, they’ll pay.
