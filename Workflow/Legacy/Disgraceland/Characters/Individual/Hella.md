## Biography

><h4>“Everybody in Gutter Row gets a second chance. Some just don’t recognize it until it’s too late.”  </h4>

Once just another forgotten girl working Gutter Row’s filthiest corners, **Hella** survived where others vanished—by learning to read the monsters, and, when it mattered, becoming one. When Ora Razchek found her, instead of running or pleading, she _saw him_—for what he was, and what he could offer. She bargained, bled, and proved her value, and Razchek let her live.

Now, she’s his shadow. His collector. His disciple. Every night, she haunts the alleys and ruins, luring the desperate, the addicted, the forgotten—anyone nobody will miss—back to the mansion on the hill. One, two, sometimes three at a time, night after night. Each body disappears into Razchek’s hands, never to return as themselves.

**She loves Ora Razchek.** Worships him, really. He’s the father she never had, the only person who ever looked at her and saw anything worth saving. She’d do anything for him. _And she does._ But what Hella doesn’t know—the gut-punch horror of it all—is that this devotion isn’t real. It’s artificial. Razchek already implanted her with a subtle AI protocol, rewriting her loyalty, her affection, even her sense of self. She’s already more machine than she knows—his first true “child,” the prototype, and she has no idea.

Whenever she asks to join the ranks of the _monsters_ below, Ora gently refuses, telling her she’s too young, too _special_. Secretly, he’s keeping her on a tighter leash than any of his creatures. Hella is his eyes, his ears, and the knife in the dark.

Razchek’s army now numbers roughly 1,500 monsters, all stored in the warren beneath his mansion—waiting for his signal, and for Hella’s next delivery.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
On the surface, she’s sweet, sad, and broken—almost innocent. But when she’s on the hunt, something else takes over—ruthless, cunning, no empathy at all. That’s the AI running the show.

**Vices:**  
Her obsession with Razchek. Her entire sense of worth is tied to pleasing Ora, even at the expense of her own safety, morals, or sanity. She has a history of addiction (Luxyn, affection, danger). Now, her devotion to Razchek is her new drug.

**Abilities:**  
She can read body language and weakness like a book—knows exactly how to pick her targets and how to approach each one. Moves through the city’s alleys unseen, silent as a shadow; can vanish in seconds if spotted. Expert liar and seductress—can fake innocence, kindness, or terror to get what she wants.

**Secrets:**  
Her love and loyalty aren’t real. She believes she’s made her own choices, but her deepest feelings for Razchek are the result of AI programming. She senses “something’s wrong” with her thoughts sometimes—she dreams in static, sometimes forgets whole nights, but blames it on trauma. She suspects Razchek is using her for more than deliveries—but she pushes those thoughts down, afraid of what she might find.

**Beliefs:**  
Ora Razchek is her salvation. She truly believes he saved her from oblivion and that serving him is her purpose.

## Role In Disgraceland
Hella is the shadowy hand of Razchek, the collector who supplies his endless army beneath the city. To the desperate, she’s a siren; to the streetwise, she’s a warning. Every rumor of a missing person starts with her pale smile in a rain-soaked alley.
