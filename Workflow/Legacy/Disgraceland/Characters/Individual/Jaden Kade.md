## Biography

><h4>“I don’t smile for ghosts. I just sing loud enough to drown ‘em out.”  </h4>

Jaden Kade was born under the rusted towers of South [[Charspire]] — the son of a failed steelworker and a mother who vanished into the factories when he was seven. He grew up in alley fires and empty warehouses, learning guitar from a drunk ex-sound tech who once toured with real bands, back when real bands could tour. Jaden’s voice broke early and never quite healed right — now it drips with gravel and hurt, raw and unforgettable.

He formed **[[The Wanderers]]** as a street act, playing for scraps and coins in makeshift markets across the regions. The band caught fire in the undercurrent — word-of-mouth spread fast, and eventually they became the pulse of backroom bars and outlaw safehouses. But everything changed the night [[Morris Gable]] showed up unannounced at a [[Gutter Row]] speakeasy, leaned against the wall, and watched them play three full sets. The next morning, Jaden was offered a deal: become the house band on [[Night Time with Morris Gable]]. Food. Shelter. National exposure. And complete creative control — as long as they didn’t "go too far."

Jaden accepted. Not for fame, not for comfort — but for reach. His rebel soul still burns, but he’s learned how to play quiet in a world built to crush noise. Every time [[The Wanderers]] perform, he pushes the edge a little further — testing how much truth he can slip past the sensors. A lyric here. A look there. One day, maybe more.

He despises Morris — calls him “the vulture in velvet” when he’s drunk — but they have an unspoken understanding. Morris gets the ratings. Jaden gets the stage. And Disgraceland gets the only real heartbeat left on the air.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Reckless but calculated. Passionate but tightly wound. Keeps his rage buried under smirks and silence until the mic’s hot — then he lets the notes bleed it out. Doesn’t say much during interviews. Keeps his answers vague, his tone flat. The fire comes out only in the music.

**Vices:**  
Stims and chews (to stay focused on long broadcast nights). Often disappears into Gutter Row for days at a time with no explanation. Has an unresolved habit of sabotaging other artists he believes are regime puppets.

**Abilities:**  
Plays multiple instruments (guitar, synth, harmonica, lapsteel).  Voice like “torn velvet soaked in whiskey” — unmistakable. Writes all The Wanderers' songs, many of which are laced with coded anti-Duke metaphors. Extremely persuasive when he wants to be.

**Secrets:**  
Jaden’s older brother was executed by Redforce in Charspire years ago — part of a failed uprising. He’s been slowly funneling slugs earned from the show toward funding illicit broadcasts and pirate signals.

**Beliefs:**  
The Duke is a coward who hides behind glass and screens. Music is more than sound — it’s memory, it’s code, it’s war. He knows Morris Gable is a liar, but he respects the man’s _control_. Even snakes have rhythm.

## Role Details
**Jaden Kade** is the unspoken voice of unrest in Disgraceland — a rebel hiding in plain sight, using the nation’s most-watched stage to echo resistance through rhythm. To the regime, he’s just the singer on a late-night show; to the people, he’s the last song that still feels honest.
