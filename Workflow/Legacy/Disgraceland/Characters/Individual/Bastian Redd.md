## Biography

><h4>“This is the last time. Win or lose, only one of us walks forward.”  </h4>

**[[Bastian Redd]]** was born beneath the blood-orange sky of the Bloodroot Canyons, where legends say the rocks remember every soul buried in the sand. Orphaned young and raised by wandering kinfolk and quiet storms, he learned to fight not in gyms or rings, but on cliff edges, under torchlight, with nothing but bone and grit. He carries the silence of that land with him — heavy, holy, and unforgiving.

Painted in sacred ochre and bearing the sigils of forgotten tribes, Bastian doesn’t just step into the [[World Lucha Libre|WLL]] ring — he _arrives_ like an omen. Wrapped fists. Bare chest. Eyes like fire before a lightning strike. His entrance is a ritual. His opponents don’t just lose — they fall like sinners in a sermon. No music. No pyrotechnics. Just that red pouch swaying on his neck — rumored to contain the ashes of someone important, though no one dares ask who.

He’s not chasing fame, belts, or broadcast deals. He’s fighting because something calls him to. Some say it’s ancestral duty. Others think he’s haunted by a vision he won’t speak aloud. One thing is certain: when [[Bastian Redd]] fights, the whole arena feels it in their teeth — like thunder rolling under their ribs.

**Bastian Redd** is a towering native of the Bloodroot Canyons, in west [[Threshton]]. Standing 6'8" and weighing 325 lbs, he's a mountain of a man — stoic, scarred, and spiritual. He entered the [[World Lucha Libre|WLL]] late in life after dominating outlaw pits and tribal gauntlets. His presence in the ring is mythic. War drums echo as he walks barefoot through the arena, red ochre paint across his shoulders, chest bare, face marked in ritual. Chanting crowds fall into reverent silence as he steps into the light.

He has no entourage. No mask. No gimmicks. Just a purpose.

**Record:** 94-3  
**Losses:**

- One clean loss to [[Bobby Cutman]] in a brutal, close match
    
- One controversial DQ (many call it rigged) — also to Cutman
    
- One early-career loss in an outlaw fight
    

Now, with a third and final title shot sanctioned by [[Flint Garritt]] himself, Bastian returns not just to fight — but to finish something that never ended.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Bastian Redd is slow to speak and quick to move. He’s quiet, focused, and always composed, like a man who’s already visualized every outcome before he steps into the ring. He doesn’t flinch, doesn’t taunt, and doesn’t play for the crowd — he fights like he’s in a sacred rite, because to him, he is.

**Vices:**  
He holds grudges the way others hold breath — deep, long, and silent. He doesn’t trust the [[World Lucha Libre|WLL]] or the business behind it, refusing all sponsorships, public interviews, or endorsements. Off-camera, he lives simply and avoids the spotlight. But those who cross him — especially those who disrespect the ceremony of the fight — don’t get second chances.

**Abilities:**  
Bastian’s strength is raw and terrifying, but it’s his composure that truly breaks opponents. He never rushes. Every strike is intentional, every grapple exact. He can weather pain with inhuman endurance and finish fights in silence. More than once, he’s been accused of fighting in a trance — like he’s somewhere else entirely.

**Secrets:**  
He never speaks of his childhood, but some claim he was trained in a hidden tradition that fuses combat with spiritual vision. He wears a small red leather pouch around his neck at all times — no one knows what’s inside. And before every fight, he vanishes for three days with no explanation, no handlers, no word.

**Beliefs:**  
Bastian believes pain is a sacred offering and that a true fight is a test of spirit, not just muscle. To him, wrestling is not sport — it is war ritual. He sees [[Bobby Cutman]] not as a rival, but as the only man capable of completing the prophecy he believes was given to him by the land itself. He does not fear death in the ring. He expects it.

## Role In Disgraceland
Bastian Redd is the immovable force returning to test the unstoppable champion. He’s the only man who’s ever shaken [[Bobby Cutman]] — and in this final match, he intends to bring more than pain. He intends to bring meaning.
