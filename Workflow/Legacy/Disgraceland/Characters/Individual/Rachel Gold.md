## Biography

><h4>“You don’t scare me. I’ve been threatened by better men, and they all ended up in my footnotes.”  </h4>

**Rachel Gold** was born in a pressroom — ink on her hands before she could write. Her parents ran an underground news flyer during the early years of the Loyalist consolidation, smuggling stories across borders and burning presses before raids. When the Tetra bomb fell, her father disappeared and her mother went silent. Rachel never stopped chasing the story.

Now, she writes for the **[[Tribucia Tribute]]**, the last printed paper in Disgraceland. Most reporters just rewrite Loyalist briefings or publish puff pieces about grain yields. Not Rachel. She digs. She exposes. She names names. Her coverage of **[[Gorren Brile|Mayor Brile]]’s torture camps**, **[[Sgt. Rottwyler]]’s black market raids**, and **slave trafficking in [[Gutter Row]]** made her both a hero and a target.

Her column, _Gold’s Truth_, runs front page more days than not. And every time it does, someone in power loses sleep.

Her editors constantly warn her to **stay away from The Duke**, and so far, she has — _publicly_. But there’s a folder buried in her filing cabinet. A thick one. With red string and ink stains. And one day, that story’s going to break.

And when it does…  
Disgraceland won’t be ready for it.

## Personality

**Temperament:**  
Quick-witted, relentless, and unapologetically direct. She's got no patience for bullshit, small talk, or weak coffee. She’ll listen to your story — but only if you get to the point.

**Vices:**  
Caffeine, long nights, confrontation. Smokes only when she’s about to publish something risky. Keeps a loaded recorder in her boot and a pair of brass knuckles in her desk drawer — just in case.

**Abilities:**  
Master investigator — knows how to bribe, charm, or blackmail the truth out of anyone. Carries an old, beat-up press badge that’s saved her life more than once. Can type a full article in one sitting, no edits needed.

**Secrets:**  
Keeps a hidden file on The Duke — dozens of leads, names, and clues no one’s allowed to see. Has an informant inside The Silo, though even she doesn’t know their real name.

**Beliefs:**  
The truth matters — even if no one believes it

## Role In Disgraceland
Rachel Gold is Disgraceland’s **last real reporter** — a fast-talking, hard-punching journalist who digs where she shouldn’t and prints what no one else will. She’s the kind of woman who asks the dangerous questions, writes the answers in bold, and _signs her name at the bottom_. Her exposés have rattled mayors, exposed corruption, and nearly got her killed more than once. She’s beloved by the public, hated by the powerful, and barely tolerated by her own editors.
