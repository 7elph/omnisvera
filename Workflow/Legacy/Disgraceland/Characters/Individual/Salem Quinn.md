## Biography

><h4>“I see clearer than the ones who build thrones. I see what waits behind the mirror. And brother… it’s already moving.”  </h4>

 
No one knows where **Salem Quinn** was born, and he doesn't offer the story. Some say he was once a scholar in the Old Capitol, a physician-psychic hybrid whose insight was so precise it bordered on supernatural. Others claim he emerged from the Ash Dunes after the Tetra Bomb fell, eyes burned shut by divine wrath. What is known is this: he walks north, alone, wearing the same blood-red suit and carrying a gold-handled cane that never strikes a pebble wrong. His eyes are pale, milked-over, but unscarred. He is blind — completely — yet he never stumbles.

Salem’s visions began long before the world was cracked open. He saw fire in the skies as a boy. Saw fields drown, people vanish, and cities become bones. He didn’t understand them then. He tried to ignore them. Tried to live small, quiet. But the visions didn’t stop — they sharpened. Became louder. Now they come in fits, in dreams, in static whispers between heartbeats. Sometimes they leave him convulsing. Sometimes just weeping. He keeps walking anyway.

He has no faction, no banners, no weapons. And yet wherever he goes, people move aside. Even gang lords and smugglers hush their tongues when he passes. In every town between **Gutter Row** and **Ashmoor**, someone always recognizes him. They call him _The Blind Seer_, or _The Walking Warning_. Children bring him water. Elders beg him for insight. He answers rarely, and only in riddles.

But Quinn isn’t wandering. He’s on a mission — one only he seems to fully understand. His goal is singular: gain an audience with **[[The Duke of Disgraceland]]**. To stand before the tyrant in his Silo throne and deliver the words that burn behind his blind eyes. Whether the message is salvation or damnation, even Quinn isn’t sure. But he knows this: if the Duke ignores it, something worse than war is coming.

Some say Salem has met **[[Veerah]]**, the prophet of the Unseen Widows, and that the two shared a vision that nearly broke them both. Others whisper that Salem already knows how Disgraceland ends — he’s just giving the world a chance to prove him wrong.

## Personality

> [!infobox]
>
> ![[sqq.png|300]]

**Temperament:**  
Quiet. Unshakably calm. His words come slow, each one measured like they cost something. He doesn’t argue. He just speaks — and lets the silence do the rest.

**Vices:**  
None apparent. He neither eats in public nor asks for shelter. Some swear he walks barefoot through snow and glass. Others say he weeps blood when no one is watching. Truth is, nobody’s sure where he sleeps… or if he sleeps at all.

**Abilities:**  
Blind, yet walks without assistance — even through minefields and ruins. Receives visions in dreams or seizures — often apocalyptic, symbolic, or maddeningly cryptic. Can sense a person’s emotional state through voice and posture alone.

**Secrets:**  
Has seen visions of the Tetra bomb before it fell. Shares a psychic resonance with [[Veerah]] — though neither one fully understands it. - His blindness may not be natural. Rumors suggest it was _taken_ from him — as payment.

**Beliefs:**  
Disgraceland’s time is nearly up

## Role In Disgraceland
**Salem Quinn** is a prophet out of time — a man blind to the world, yet cursed to see too much. He travels slowly but purposefully, walking from the rot of [[GUTTER ROW]] toward the tyranny of [[Ashmoor]], seeking one thing: an audience with [[The Duke]]. His message is unknown, but the look on his face suggests it’s already too late.
