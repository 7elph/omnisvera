## Biography

><h4>“They call me quiet like it’s weakness. But quiet people hear everything.”</h4>

Davis grew up working the sunburnt ranchlands of Threshton, the youngest of five boys. His family wasn’t loyal to the Duke—just scared enough to pretend they were. His mother died during a fuel riot when he was nine, trampled by panicked townsfolk. His father was arrested two years later for hoarding “unbranded slugs,” and Davis never saw him again.

He was a quiet kid, smarter than most gave him credit for, with a knack for observing without speaking. People underestimated him, and he learned to survive by making himself small—until the night everything shattered.

The night the Duke’s men raided Threshton under the guise of “security enforcement,” Davis was taken from his bunk, shackled, and thrown into a supply skiff bound for Ashmoor. Emily Burton was in the same transport, though they didn’t know each other then. Some of the others fought back. They didn’t survive the trip.

Once in Ashmoor, Davis was handpicked—not because he was strong, but because he was pretty. The Duke likes his pets obedient and quiet. Davis was branded, collared, and renamed "Pollard" in the house records. But in his mind, he never let go of his real name.

He became a ghost in the halls of The Dead Waters estate, tending to the Duke’s personal quarters, witnessing horrors no one should see, never allowed to speak unless spoken to. But through the years, he began learning secrets—whispers between the walls, the routines of guards, the Duke’s hidden fears.

Davis isn’t a fighter. Not yet. But there’s something simmering beneath that quiet exterior. A seed of rebellion. He hasn’t forgotten who he is. And when the time comes, he may be the one holding the key—literally or figuratively—to taking the whole rotten empire down from the inside.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Quiet, observant, and deeply internal — Davis rarely speaks, but his silence isn’t fear. It’s focus.
**Vices:**  
Self-blame. He carries every tragedy like it was his fault, even when it wasn’t. Especially when it wasn’t.
**Abilities:**  
Expert eavesdropper and silent mover — Davis can memorize patrols, read lips, and vanish into shadow. He’s not trained for war, but he’s trained for survival in a gilded prison.
**Secrets:**  
Tucked under a loose floorboard in his quarters is a scrap of cloth from his mother’s apron, wrapped around a key he stole from a distracted guard. He doesn’t know what it opens — yet.
**Beliefs:**  
He doesn’t believe in heroes. But he _does_ believe in moments — that one right moment can change everything. And when his comes, he plans to be ready.

## Role In Disgraceland
**Davis Pollard** is a prisoner in silk sheets, a nobody in the Duke’s house who sees _everything._ He may not look like a threat… but one whisper in the right ear, one door unlocked at the wrong time, and he might be the one who ends it all.
