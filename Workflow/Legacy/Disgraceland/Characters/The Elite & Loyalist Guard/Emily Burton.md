## Biography

><h4>“They dressed me in silk and called it salvation. But silk burns too.”</h4>

They call her The Duchess, but she never asked for that name.

Born just outside the ruins of Lansing to a family of tinkerers and scavengers, Emily Burton was bright, curious, and impossibly bold from a young age. Her father, a former engineer from before the blast, was part of a quiet circle rebuilding pre-Tetra tech in secret. Her mother kept a hidden school for the neighboring children. The Burtons were beloved by their community, but too smart, too outspoken, too dangerous for [[The Duke]]'s vision of order.

When Emily was sixteen, the [[Loyalist Guard]]s came.

[[The Duke]] offered the Burtons a choice: surrender their work and serve him… or be erased. Emily watched from the floorboards as her parents were executed in their home. She was taken in chains to [[Ashmoor]], where The Duke presented her on broadcast as his ward — a girl "rescued from chaos" by the guiding hand of Disgraceland’s ruler. Months later, he declared her his wife. His Duchess. It was theater. A grotesque display of dominance. The Duke, impotent and delusional, sought legacy through image, not blood.

Now 22, Emily walks the halls of [[The Silo]] Mansion like a ghost in silk. She smiles for the cameras. She stands beside him on the balcony during broadcasts. Her voice is never heard — only her presence. But behind those blue eyes is a mind sharpened on silence. She is not broken. She is waiting. Watching.

Emily understands the power her beauty holds. She knows the whispers in the halls, the jealous eyes of Loyalist wives, the quiet awe from the poor who see her as a living doll in a white dress. But she also knows that The Silo protects her, even if it’s a cage. There’s a bed of secrets under this place — guards who owe her favors, servants who tell her what they hear, a quiet network of sympathy she has cultivated one kind word at a time.

She hates The Duke. Hates his hands, his voice, his fake faith. But she knows his routines. She knows when he sleeps. She knows what lies beneath the grain silo. And she knows that someday, if Disgraceland is to change... she might be the one to light the match.

Emily Burton — _The Duchess_ in name only — is a poised, calculating survivor wrapped in the illusion of obedience. Raised by brilliant rebels and forced into a gilded cage by the man who slaughtered her family, she wears her silence like a blade honed by years of observation. She plays her role flawlessly: a porcelain figurehead in silk, offering smiles while quietly weaving a web of influence through whispered alliances and owed favors. Emily’s beauty is her camouflage, her intellect her weapon. She studies everyone — their habits, their weaknesses — waiting for the moment to strike. Her secret? Hidden in a locked drawer behind the mirror in her private chamber is a small brass pendant… the last creation her father ever built, which — unknown to all — contains a detailed schematic of The Silo’s underground network. She's not just waiting for revolution. She's designing it.

## Personality:
**Temperament:**  
Calm, observant, and disarmingly gentle — Emily wields restraint like a blade, rarely showing anger but always calculating outcomes two steps ahead.  
**Vices:**  
Obsessive control. She rehearses her every move, word, and smile until perfection, terrified that one misstep will crack the mask and cost her everything.  
**Abilities:**  
Silently adept at manipulating people through subtle charm and emotional read.  
**Secrets:**  
She possesses a miniature pendant containing a full schematic of the Silo’s understructure — smuggled and salvaged from her father's hidden workshop the night of his execution.  
**Beliefs:**  
Emily no longer believes in saviors — only leverage. But deep down, she clings to her mother’s whispered lesson: “Knowledge is the only freedom worth dying for.”

## Role In Disgraceland
**Emily Burton** is the silent symbol of the regime — paraded as The Duke’s dutiful Duchess, but in truth, a brilliant prisoner biding her time. She plays the part of perfection while quietly gathering the pieces to burn it all down.
