## Biography

> “Words are just bullets with better aim, baby. And I don’t miss.”


**Tren Knight**, better known on the streets as **“Ninepack,”** is the slick-talking, sharp-dressing broker of [[The Third Row]] and the blood kin of [[Samson Knight]] — his little cousin, but nobody calls him that if they like their teeth.

With the looks of a prophet and the tongue of a hustler, Baby Nine was never built for the alleys or the blade. But he could sell heat to Charspire and talk a Loyalist guard into handing over their badge. From the time he was twelve, he was running side hustles, love notes, and dirty credits through the pipes of [[Vileby]], always one move ahead of his enemies and two steps behind the girls.

While [[Jailface]] cleans up messes, Baby Nine *prevents* them — or makes sure they turn a profit when they happen. He organizes the tribute flow, fences high-value goods, manages outside syndicate communications, and smooths tensions between rival crews with charm, smoke, and veiled threats. His connection to Mr. Knight gets him access, but his silver tongue keeps him indispensable.

He’s a poet when drunk, a savage when crossed, and a legend in bed — if his rhymes are to be believed.

## Personality

**Temperament:** 
Charismatic, fast-talking, smooth under pressure. Loves attention, but rarely loses control.

**Vices:** 
Women, wordplay, and private stashes of imported liquor. Keeps a book of poems he’s never shown anyone.

**Abilities:** 
Deal-making, double-speak, blackmail, negotiation. Can read a mark in seconds and flip them in minutes.

**Secrets:** 
Ninepack is rumored to be running quiet talks with a Threshton contact, despite Knight’s warnings. Whether it’s treason or business... only he knows.

**Beliefs:** 
Everything can be negotiated. Everyone wants something — your job is to find out what and charge double.

## Role in Disgraceland
Ninepack is the velvet tongue of The Third Row — a street poet with hustler instincts who makes sure the market never stops spinning, and no deal ever closes without a cut for Mr. Knight.
