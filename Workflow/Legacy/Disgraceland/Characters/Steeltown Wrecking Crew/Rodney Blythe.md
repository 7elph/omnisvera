## Biography

><h4>“I don’t need to shout. The pavement hears me just fine.”</h4>

**Rodney Blythe** doesn’t talk much. He doesn’t need to. His silence speaks volumes, and his fists do the rest. Built like a slab of steel and just as unyielding, Rodney is the wall you want between your people and the storm.

Once a prisoner in the Duke’s forced labor camps, Rodney spent years beneath rubble and ash, hauling wreckage from bombed-out ruins. When a collapse left him buried alive, he clawed his way out. That moment — his rebirth — made him harder than any iron he ever lifted.

The Crew found him broken but breathing. [[Abel May Jr.]] saw a survivor, not a stranger, and vouched for him. Since then, Rodney has never wavered. His word is rare, but his loyalty is absolute.

As Road Captain, he’s the one who gets them home. He leads convoys through danger, enforces discipline, and settles disputes with his bare hands. If Jimmy is the spirit, and Mark is the voice, Rodney is the spine. Without him, the Crew would buckle under its own weight.

He’s not without ghosts — and he never speaks of the past. But in quiet moments, a bent dog tag hidden in his vest says everything he won’t.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Steady, focused, and silent — Rodney listens first, acts second, and regrets nothing.
**Vices:**  
Carries his guilt like weight on his back — never complains, never lets it go.
**Abilities:**  
Route strategist, convoy lead, close-combat specialist — Rodney plans like a general and hits like a truck.
**Secrets:**  
A dog tag hidden in his vest once belonged to someone who didn’t make it out. He checks it before every ride.
**Beliefs:**  
Strength is protection, not power. Loyalty is a language. And the road is sacred — you never abandon the ride, or the riders.

## Role In Disgraceland
**Rodney Blythe** is the Wrecking Crew’s steel spine — the Road Captain who guides the charge, clears the path, and brings the warriors home.
