## Biography

><h4>“I don’t ride to escape, man. I ride to remember what we’re trying to get back to.”</h4>

**Lincoln Jones** lives in motion. The hum of his engine is the only rhythm that keeps the world from going silent — and he won’t stop riding until he hears music again.

To most, he’s a mystery. A drifter with a grin, a box of busted cassettes, and a knack for vanishing into the wilds only to return with intel, supplies, or static-laced mixtapes from a world long gone. But to the Crew, he’s more than that — he’s their Road Dog. Their outrider. Their reminder that there’s still something worth riding for.

Lincoln saved [[Katie Kowalski]] once, and she never forgot it. That moment was his quiet initiation. But it was [[Joe Ameen]] who taught him the finer art of bringing old tech back to life. Together, they dug through ruins and rewired speakers that hadn’t sung in decades. Lincoln’s dream? To build a sound system loud enough to shake the Duke’s towers. Just once. Just for the hell of it.

When he rides, he scouts ahead. When he stops, he listens. And when he speaks, it’s with the steady calm of someone who’s seen too much and still believes in joy anyway.

## Personality
**Temperament:**  
Relaxed and open-hearted, but observant — Lincoln may seem carefree, but he doesn’t miss a thing.
**Vices:**  
Sentimental to a fault — he’ll risk his life for a scratched-up CD if it reminds him of something real.
**Abilities:**  
Elite scout, improvised mechanic, cook, and battlefield courier. Fast as hell, smart as hell, and always humming something you thought you forgot.
**Secrets:**  
Carries a broken CD marked _“Mom’s Mix – Road Trip 2040.”_ It’s scratched to hell and doesn’t play. But he listens to the silence like it’s a prayer.
**Beliefs:**  
The road is sacred. The past isn’t lost. And sometimes, the best way to rebel is to make the world feel something again — even if it’s just a beat.

## Role In Disgraceland
**Lincoln Jones** is the Crew’s wind — their scout, their soul, their rolling reminder that freedom ain’t just an idea — it’s a ride. And he’ll ride until the wheels fall off or the music comes back.
