## Biography

><h4>“The difference between a secret and a weapon is what I’m willing to do with it.”</h4>

Nobody voted for [[Cricket Granger]]. Hell, most folks in [[GUTTER ROW]] didn’t even know he was a candidate. One day, he was just there—lounging in the velvet chair behind the mayor’s desk, drink in one hand, bruised lipstick on his neck, smiling like a cat in a slaughterhouse.

People whisper he caught [[The Duke]] of Disgraceland in a compromising position deep in the red-lit halls of Gutter Row years ago. A drunken night, a wrong room, maybe a lover with the wrong kind of secrets. And Cricket? He kept the receipt. Blackmail? Leverage? Who knows. What’s certain is this: no one touches Cricket Granger.

Now he’s the Mayor of Gutter Row, and his "office" is the most depraved whorehouse in [[DISGRACELAND]].

He doesn’t hide it. He flaunts it.

His estate is a den of velvet couches, incense smoke, and moaning voices behind silk curtains. The place stinks of perfume, sweat, and something darker. Sex work isn’t just permitted in [[GUTTER ROW]]—it’s government-funded. Everyone knows if you walk into the mayor’s estate, you’re not there for permits.

Granger’s tastes are limitless and lawless. Men, women, non-binary, twins, masked figures, chained lovers—he’s seen it all, done most of it, and talks about it like he’s sharing tea. He’ll shake your hand with the same fingers that were inside someone fifteen minutes earlier and never break eye contact.

But he’s not just a pervert.  
He’s a survivor.

[[Cricket Granger]] knows everything. Every secret, every kink, every betrayal. He’s got dirt on guards, soldiers, mayors, ranchers—hell, maybe even the [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew]]. He doesn’t need a militia. He’s got blackmail and blowjobs as weapons. He runs [[GUTTER ROW]] not by force, but through deals, favors, and fear of exposure.

Criminals leave him alone. So do Loyalists. Even [[Gorren Brile]] steps carefully when he visits the Row, watching the shadows, knowing Cricket might’ve seen him beg once.

And through it all, Granger smiles—like he’s always two steps ahead, like he’s already imagining how you’ll look naked and crying.

But even rats drown when the sewer floods.  
And in [[GUTTER ROW]], storm clouds are gathering.
