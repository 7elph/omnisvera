#  [[Steeltown Wrecking Crew|Wrecking Crew]] Clubhouse


> [!NOTE|clean no-i right]+ ‎CLUBHOUSE
> ![[club.png|500]]  

## Overview

The Steeltown Wrecking Crew Clubhouse is more than just a hideout — it’s the beating heart of [[Steeltown]]. Located at the edge of the old steel yards where the smokestacks still claw the sky, the clubhouse is equal parts fortress, bar, mechanic’s garage, and war room. This is where the Wrecking Crew drinks, plans, fights, mourns, fucks, laughs, and bleeds.

The building itself is a reinforced two-story bunker welded together from leftover industrial scrap and old union headquarters. The front is decked out with sandbags, floodlights, and a steel gate painted with the Crew’s black-and-yellow skull emblem. The inside is a dense jungle of makeshift furniture, riot shields mounted as trophies, oil-drum fire pits, chalkboards covered in supply requests, and racks of weapons from every era.

---

### Notable Features

- **The Frontroom**: Acts as both swillpot and reception. Locals can drink here under the Crew’s watchful eye, but troublemakers don’t last long. The bar is made from a molten slab of steel once part of a factory press, still warm to the touch.
    
- **The Pit**: A below-ground fighting ring used for private brawls, punishments, and Crew rites. Blood is washed off with jet hoses, if at all.
    
- **The Bay**: Workshop and garage space where Crew mechanics work on bikes, rigs, and armored haulers. Smells like fuel and pride.
    
- **The War Table**: A steel table bolted to the floor with a custom-carved map of [[Disgraceland]], used for planning raids, convoys, and defenses.
    
- **The Shrine**: A modest wall dedicated to fallen Crew members. Each name is hand-carved into metal plates, some with bullet holes or scorch marks.

### History

The Steeltown Wrecking Crew Clubhouse wasn’t always a fortress. Back in 2171, it was just a broken-down garage in [[STEELTOWN#Gage Park|Gage Park]], half-collapsed and crawling with rust. But [[Jimmy Douglass]] saw potential in it — more than that, he saw a future. With grit, salvaged steel, and a vision that most folks would’ve laughed at, he staked his claim.

Jimmy started with a single room and a small bay, sleeping beside his bike with a shotgun by the door. Over time, and with the help of men he trusted — [[Mark Jeffrey]], [[Rodney Blythe]], and [[Joe Ameen]] — the place evolved. Every nail, every plate of armor, every bottle behind the bar has their fingerprints on it.

Over the next fifteen years, the Wrecking Crew turned that crumbling garage into a landmark of survival and defiance. They built a full meeting room where strategy and shouting match in equal measure. Barracks to house the new generation of wreckers. Five private bedrooms, one for each of the founding builders. A den and swillpot where locals now drink under the Crew’s watchful eyes. And a massive garage where [[Joe Ameen]] still fixes bikes like he’s tuning up warhorses.

The clubhouse isn’t a relic. It’s alive. And it still belongs to the Crew, the same way it always did.

Even [[Ellis Westmount]], the Duke’s appointed mayor, doesn’t step foot inside without an escort. The Crew doesn’t answer to him — they never did.

### Vibe

Raucous, grimy, brotherly. This place oozes loyalty and tension. Every sound echoes off metal. Every drink feels like it might be your last. And every soul inside knows damn well that if the Duke ever wanted Steeltown under control, this would be the first place to burn.
