# Tribucia Tribute

> [!infobox]
>
> ![[paper.png|300]]

## Overview  
The *Tribucia Tribute* is the largest and most widely read newspaper in [[TRIBUCIA MAP|Disgraceland]]. Though not officially mandatory, it’s printed overnight and delivered to homes and businesses every morning — making it a daily presence in nearly every region.

Originally founded as a beacon of free journalism, the paper has since become a tool of subtle propaganda under the watchful eye of [[The Duke]]. Direct criticism of the regime is forbidden, though occasional subversive truths still slip through — especially when reported by the paper’s most famous journalist.

---

## Ownership  
The paper is owned by [[Royce Sincayd]], a once-principled journalist turned political opportunist. Royce started the *Tribucia Tribute* out of a genuine love for storytelling, public truth, and accountability. Over time, he succumbed to political pressure and now cooperates directly with Loyalist Forces to ensure all Duke-facing articles are filtered or adjusted to maintain “order.”

Royce is not hated by the public — but nobody really trusts him either.

---

## Key Staff  

- **[[Rachel Gold]]** — The paper’s uncontested star reporter. She’s known across Tribucia as a folk hero. Brilliant, fearless, and relentless in her pursuit of truth. Her stories always find the beating heart of a crisis, even if she has to dig it out with her bare hands.  
  Despite the risk, [[Royce Sincayd]] is often forced to run her stories front and center, knowing the public demands it. Rachel constantly tests the paper’s boundaries and frequently butts heads with Royce over censorship.

- **[[Marlon Lambert]]** — A capable, methodical journalist who lives in [[Rachel Gold]]’s shadow. Marlon lacks her charisma but is quietly respected. He often cleans up after her, does follow-ups on her investigations, and keeps her safe when things get risky. He won’t say it, but he’s protective of her — maybe too much.

---

## Recent Controversy  
A recent front-page piece by [[Rachel Gold]] broke down the new fuel tax imposed by [[The Duke]], detailing how it disproportionately hurts working citizens while funneling wealth into Ashmoor. Though the article never directly attacked The Duke, the numbers spoke for themselves.  

The fallout was immediate: protests, ration hoarding, and quiet investigations into Rachel’s past sources. [[Royce Sincayd]] nearly fired her, but public backlash forced him to keep her in place — for now.

---

## Public Reputation  
The *Tribucia Tribute* is both trusted and distrusted. Most people read it because there’s no real alternative. But many know to read between the lines, especially in [[Rachel Gold]]’s stories.  

Her byline signals something worth reading.  
Royce’s editorials usually signal something worth ignoring.  
Nobody talks about Marlon’s stories — but everybody reads them.
